New Words

weekday
n.any day except Sunday and Saturday
工作日（星期六、日以外的日子）
That store opens only on weekdays.
这商店只在工作日营业。

click
vi.make a short, sharp sound
发出"嘟"、"咔嗒"等轻微响声
The key clicked in the lock.
钥匙在琐里"咔嗒"响了一声。
n.a short, sharp sound
咔嗒声
The door opened with a click.
门"咔嗒"一声开了。

v.1. make a sudden, loud sound
发出猛烈响声
As she entered the room, the sound of western music blasted forth.
她进房间时，猛地响起了西部音乐。
2. break up by explosion
爆破
They blasted a hole in the wall so that they could get through.
他们在墙上炸了一个洞以进入屋内。
3. attack with explosives
以炸药攻击
The soldiers blasted the stronghold.
战士们用炸药炸碉堡。

咱们走前把这东西带上。

Language Points

Language Point 1
The radio clicked on. Rock music blasted forth. (Para. 1)
Meaning: As soon as the radio started with a short, sharp sound, rock music began loudly.
click: (1) v. make a short, sharp sound
                  The key clicked in the lock. 钥匙在锁里 "咔嗒" 响了一声。
        (2) n. a short, sharp sound
                The door closed with a click. 门 "咔嗒"一声关了。
blast: v. make a sudden, loud sound
She was so angry that she blasted him with insults and loudly mentioned all his faults.  她气愤之极，恶声恶气地辱骂他，并大声把他的短处数落殆尽。

Language Point 2
Like a shot, the music woke Sandy. (Para. 1)
Meaning: Sandy heard the music and woke up as if startled by a gunshot.
Please notice that these three words -"click", "blast" and "shot"- in the first three sentences are used to suggest a sense of suddenness.

Language Point 3
Sandy sang along with the words as she lay listening to her favorite radio station. (Para. 1)
Meaning: Sandy followed the words of the song although she was still in bed, listening to her favorite radio station.
along with: in company with, together with
I'll go along with you. 我和你一道去。
There was a bill along with the letters. 信里还附着一份账单。
"As" in the sentence here is used as a conjunction, meaning "while".
As she ran to catch the school bus, Sandy thought of her brother. 在赶校车时，桑迪想起了哥哥。

Language Point 4
... turn that music off! (Para. 2)
  Means: ... stop that music!
  turn off: stop
  Never forget to turn off the lights of your car after you park, or you won't be able to start your car. 停车后千万不要忘记关上灯，否则你就不能发动汽车了。

Language Point 5
Steve Finch burst into her room. (Para. 2)
Meaning: Steve Finch rushed into her room without knocking or warning her in advance.
burst into: (1) enter hurriedly or explosively
                    The children burst into the room looking for toys. 孩子们冲进房间找玩具。
                (2) start suddenly
                    She burst into laughter. 她突然大笑了起来。

Language Point 6
It's the same thing over and over. (Para. 2)
Meaning: You listen to the same music again and again.
  over and over：again and again, repeatedly
  Read the word over and over until you do it right. 反复读这个单词，直到你读对了为止。
  I have told them over and over again, so they know what to do. 我反复跟他们说了好多次了，他们知道该做什么。
  Notice that you can say "over and over" or "again and again", or "over and over again", but never say "again and over".

Language Point 7
... though it does have rhythm. (Para. 2)
Meaning: ... though it really has rhythm.
rhythm: n. regular repeated pattern of sounds or movements
  That music has a strong rhythm. 这音乐的节奏很强。
  Here "does" is used for emphasis (强调) and should be followed by the bare infinitive form of the verb.
  He does have a brother in England. 他的确有个弟弟在英国。

Language Point 8
It is definitely horrible stuff. (Para. 2)
Meaning: The music is certainly very bad.
definitely: ad. certainly, with no doubt
Without enough teachers the education in this area is definitely a problem; no one can argue that it is not serious. 没有足够的师资，这个地区的教育肯定成问题，没有人能否认这个问题的严重性。
horrible: a. awful, unpleasant
What a horrible mistake you've made! 你犯了个多么可怕的错误！

Language Point 9
... it's my favorite group — Green Waves. (Para. 3)
Meaning：I like this musical group best. Its name is Green Waves.
Here "group" refers to a band (乐队), that is, a group of musicians.

Language Point 10
It has a really powerful message. (Para. 3)
Meaning: The music is really meaningful.
message: n. a lesson or moral contained in a story, a film, a piece of music, or a speech
The message of the story "A Busy Weekday Morning" is that there is a generation gap between the old and the young. 短篇小说《一个繁忙的早晨》说的是老年人与青年人之间存在着代沟。

Language Point 11
Sandy reached for the radio to turn it up louder. (Para. 3)
Meaning: Sandy stretched her hand out to increase the volume of the radio.
  reach for: stretch one's hand so as to touch or hold something
  In "reach for", "reach" is used as an intransitive verb.
  There was no time for me to reach for my gun. 我没有时间伸手拿枪。
  Please notice that the verb "reach" is often used as a transitive verb, meaning
(1) get to, come to, arrive at
You can see the house when you reach the top of the hill. 到了山顶上，你就能看到那座房子了。
Your letter reached me yesterday. 我昨天收到了你的信。
(2)  touch, get in touch with
I am short and cannot reach the top of the wall. 我个子矮，摸不到墙顶。
turn up: increase (a flame, a sound, etc.) usually by moving a switch
We turned up the heat to make the room warmer. 我们把暖气调高使房间更加暖和。

Language Point 12
I can't stand it. (Para. 4)
  Meaning: I cannot bear it.
  stand: vt. endure, bear
  Here "stand" is used as a transitive verb, but it's not as formal as "bear".
  I can't stand her makeup. 我不能忍受她化的妆。
  He can stand more pain than anyone else I know. 他比我所知道的任何人都更能忍受痛苦。

Language Point 13
... and the musicians didn't use such offensive language. (Para. 4)
  Meaning: ... and the musicians didn't use such bad language.
  offensive: a. bad, very unpleasant
  The room has an offensive smell. Why not open the window and air it?  房间的味道难闻死了，干嘛不开窗透透气？

Language Point 14
Turn that radio down ...(Para. 4)
  Meaning: Reduce the sound of the radio ...
  turn down: reduce (a flame, a sound, etc.) usually by moving a switch
  Please turn the television down. I'm trying to sleep.
  请把电视机关小点声，我想睡觉了。

Language Point 15
Now, would you please hurry up and turn it off? (Para. 4)
  Meaning: Now, would you please move faster and turn the radio off?
  hurry up: move or do something faster
  Hurry up, children, the taxi's waiting outside! 孩子们，快点，出租车在外面等着呢！

Language Point 16
... and turned on the shower. (Para. 5)
  Meaning: ... and switched on the shower.
  turn on: switch on, cause to flow or operate
  When I come home from work in the evenings, sometimes I am too tired to even turn on the television. 我晚上下班回家，有时累得连电视也不想打开。
  Up to now we have learned four verbal phrases beginning with the verb "turn", that is, "turn on", "turn off", "turn up" and "turn down". Don't get confused (混淆) with their meanings. When you turn on a radio, it begins to produce sound. But when you turn it off, it produces no sound at all. Almost the same way, when you turn a radio up, the sound it produces gets louder. But when you turn it down, the sound becomes weaker. Actually, the phrases are two groups of antonyms (反义词), phrases of the opposite meanings: turn on and turn off; turn up and turn down.

Language Point 17
At first, the water felt cold. (Para. 5)
  Meaning: At first the water was cold  (or Sandy could feel that the water was cold).
  Please note that here "feel" is not used in the sense of "experience" as in the example of "He felt a sudden pain in his heart".
  Here "feel" means "seem" or "give or produce a certain sensation" (感觉). When "feel" is used in this sense, the subject of "feel" is something that is described by an adjective or an adverb that follows "feel".
  This shower feels great. 这淋浴的感觉好极了。
  My feet feel cold. 我的脚摸上去很冷。
  The clothes feel soft. 这衣服摸起来很柔软。

Language Point 18
It helped her wake up. (Para. 5)
  Meaning: It helped her become aware of the situation.
  wake up: (cause to) stop sleeping
  Note: "wake" is often followed by "up".
  Could you wake me (up) at about eight? 能否在8点左右叫醒我？
  I woke (up) late this morning. 今天早上我醒得晚。

Language Point 19
...her mom or dad usually banged on the door ...(Para. 5)
  Meaning: Her mom or dad would hit hard and with loud noises on the door.
  bang: v. (cause to) knock, beat, or move violently and with a loud noise
  I could hear the door banging in the wind. 我能听到门被风吹得直响。
  She banged the chair against the wall. 她把椅子猛地往墙上一撞。

Language Point 20
... so she grabbed a towel and dried off. (Para. 5)
  Meaning: she quickly took hold of a towel and dried her body with it.
  dry off:  (cause to) become dry
  The swimmer dried off in the hot sun. 游泳者在烈日下晒干身子。
  She ran in after the rainstorm and dried herself off with a thick towel. 大雨过后她跑进来，用一块厚毛巾把身子擦干。

Language Point 21
Then she put on her makeup，...(Para. 6)
  Meaning: Then she made up her face.
  We are familiar with "put on clothes, gloves or something else." Here we have learned " put on makeup".

Language Point 22
As usual, she didn't know what to have for breakfast, so she grabbed a glass of milk and ate a piece of toast while standing by the sink. (Para. 7)
  Meaning: As had happened before, she didn't know what to have for breakfast, so she quickly took a glass of milk and ate a piece of toast while she was standing by the sink.
  as usual: as is common, as has happened before与往常一样
  As usual, Sandy's music made her father's stomach feel uneasy. 与往常一样，桑迪的音乐使她爸爸忧虑不安。

Language Point 24
It isn't healthy to eat standing up. (Para. 8)
  Meaning: It isn't good to your health if you have your breakfast while standing up.

Language Point 25
"Do you have your instrument?" (Para. 12)
  Meaning: "Have you got your music instrument with you?"
  Here "instrument" refers to a musical instrument.

Language Point 26
I'll brush my teeth when I'm done. (Para. 17)
  Meaning: I'll brush my teeth when I have finished my breakfast.
  done: a. finished
  When you are done, come back. 你干完后就回来。

Language Point 27
It's disgusting. (Para. 18)
  Meaning: It's bad, and I don't like it.
  disgusting: a. very unpleasant
  What a disgusting smell! 多么难闻的气味！
  I think it's disgusting the way the government raises taxes. 我认为政府加税的做法实在可恨。

Language Point 28
Sandy pretended not to notice that her mother was a little annoyed. (Para. 23)
  Meaning: Sandy acted as if she did not notice that her mother was a little angry.
  annoy: vt. make a little angry
  The police told me to call them immediately if the man annoyed me again. 警察告诉我如果那个人再来骚扰我，就立即打电话给他们。

Language Point 29
Sandy Finch, you're too young to wear that much makeup. (Para. 24)
  Meaning: Sandy Finch, you're not old enough to wear so much makeup.
  Notice that the mother greeted the daughter by full name, which means the mother was very firm in her opinion and/or wanted to be sure to get her daughter's full attention.
  that: ad. (informal) so; to such a degree
  I didn't know you cared that much. 我不知道你这么在乎。
  I like him but not that much. 我喜欢他，但 还不至于到那种程度。
  Compare with "this": ad. (informal) so; to this degree
  You can't have this much for breakfast. 早餐你不能吃这么多。
  I've never been out this late before. 我从未在外面待得这么晚。
  Please notice that some people think it is not very good English to use "that" and "this" as adverbs; instead of "that late" or "this late", they say "as late as that" or "as late as this". In modern spoken English, however, use of "this" and "that" as adverbs is common and is considered more fluent.

Language Point 30
Please go upstairs and wash it off. (Para. 24)
Meaning: Please go upstairs and wash the makeup off.
Here "it" refers to "the makeup".

Language Point 31
... and bolted out of the house. (Para. 25)
Meaning: ... and left the house quickly.
bolt out: leave in a hurry
When I opened the door, a cat bolted out. 我打开门时，一只猫窜了出来。

Language Point 32
... but she hadn't heard from him for a while. (Para. 26)
Meaning: ... but she hadn't received any phone calls from him for quite a few days.
hear from: receive a letter or telephone call from (someone)
We were so worried when we didn't hear from you for three weeks. 三周没有你的消息，我们非常担心。
