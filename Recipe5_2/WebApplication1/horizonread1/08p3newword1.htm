1. benefit
n. 好处,利益
vt. have a good or useful effect on 有益于
  e.g. For the benefit of those people who arrived late, I'll just go over the plan again.

 

  

2. in the first place
in the beginning; to begin with 首先
  e.g. In the first place I don't want to go, and in the second place I can't afford to.

 

3. proceed
vi. go on 继续进行
  e.g. We can now proceed to the main business of the meeting.

 

  

4. competent
a. having the ability or skill needed for sth. 有能力的，能胜任的
  e.g. My secretary is perfectly competent, but she doesn't have much initiative.

 

5. essential
a. necessary; most important 必要的;最重要的
  e.g. We can live without clothes, but food and drink are essential.

 

  

6. concentrate on
direct (one's attention, efforts, etc.) towards 集中(注意力、精力等)于
  e.g. This year the company has concentrated on improving its efficiency.

 

7. solve
vt. 解决(问题、困难等)
  e.g. Can you solve this problem / this riddle?

 

  

8. effective
a. 有效的;生效的
  e.g. Their efforts to improve the school have been very effective.

 

9. confuse
vt. 使困惑
  e.g. Don't give me so much information -- you are confusing me.

 

  

10. what if
what will happen if 如果...怎么样
  e.g. What if we move the picture over there? Do you think it'll look better?

 

11. focus
v. (使)集中注意力(于)
  e.g. The beams of light moved across the sky and focused on the aircraft.

 

  

12. at hand
在手边;临近
  e.g. The great day is at hand.  

 

13. claim
vt. say that (sth.) is true or is a fact without having any proof 声称;断言
  e.g. They claimed to have discovered / They claim that they have discovered a cure for the disease, but this has not yet been proved.  

 

  

14. contribute
v. 作出贡献;贡献出
  e.g. This advertising campaign has contributed significantly to the success of the new car.

 
 

15. ultimate
a. last or final; basic 最终的;根本的
  e.g. They're going to London first, but their ultimate destination is Rome.
