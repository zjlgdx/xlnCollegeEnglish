1. cautious
a. taking great care to avoid danger 谨慎的
  e.g. The bank is very cautious about lending money.

 

  

2. display
vt. show (esp. a feeling or quality) 流露;显示;展示
n. an act of showing sth.
  e.g. She displayed great self-control when they told her the news.

 

3. accompany
vt. go with 陪伴，陪同
  e.g. Let me accompany you to your hotel.

 

  

4. exhibit
vt. display 展示
n. 展品
  e.g. The new cars were proudly exhibited in the showroom window.

 

5. typical
a. 典型的
  e.g. This painting is fairly typical of his early work.

 

  

6. pull oneself together
振作起来
  e.g. Stop acting like a baby! Pull yourself together!

 

7. purchase
vt. (fml) buy 买，购买
  e.g. They purchased life at the expense of honor.

 

  

8. nevertheless
ad.，conj. however; in spite of that 然而; 虽然如此
  e.g. He was angry; nevertheless, he listened to me.

 

9. take to
start to like 喜欢上
  e.g. Did the children take to their new teacher?

 

  

10. magnificent
a. extremely impressive and attractive; excellent 宏大的，壮观的;出色的
  e.g. The royal wedding was a magnificent occasion.

 

11. shed light on
make (sth.) clear and easy to understand 阐明
  e.g. Does this information shed any light on the problem?

 

  

12. indicate
vt. 表明
  e.g. He indicated his willingness with a nod of his head.

 

13. observe
v. see and notice; watch carefully 观察;注意
  e.g. Did you observe anything unusual in his behavior?

 

 

  

14. alarm
vt. make (sb.) feel suddenly frightened or worried 使惊慌，使恐慌 n. 惊慌，恐慌;警报
  e.g. The government is alarmed by the dramatic increase in violent crime.

 

15. reject
vt. refuse to accept 拒绝接受
  e.g. He was rejected for the army because of his bad eyesight.
