New Words

community
n.a group of people living together and/or united by the same interest, religion or nationality
社区，社会, 团体，界
The people in our community attend all the sports meetings at the high school.
住在我们社区里的人参加那所学校举办的所有运动会。

expand
v.1. (cause to) increase in size, number, degree or volume
（使）扩大，（使）扩张，(使)扩展
We expanded our house when we had a second baby.
有了第二个小孩之后，我们把房子扩大了一点。
2. grow larger
膨胀
Wooden doors expand in hot, wet weather.
木门在潮热天气会膨胀。

aware
a.having knowledge or understanding
意识到的，知道的
New babies are sometimes not aware of other people.
新生儿有时并不意识到他人的存在。
We were not aware of the rain until we went outside.
直到我们走出门才意识到下雨了。

unique
a.1. being the only one of its type
惟一的，独特的 ，独一无二的
The music of that small country is very unique.
那个小国家的音乐非常独特。
2. unusual
极不寻常的，极好的
We have developed a unique method for preparing beef.
我们发明了一种加工牛肉的好办法。

following
a.next
接着的，下述的
the five following sentences
下面五个句子
n.a group of supporters or admirers
一批追随者
He has a wide following.
他有一大批追随者。

ideal
a.1. perfect; what one like best
完满的；理想的
An ideal place for a home would be near a park.
理想的家居应靠近公园。
2. out of one's mind, not real; not practical
想像的，空想的
The ideal design would never be put into practice.
脱离实际的设计永远不会付诸实施。

visual
a.of or gained by sight
视觉的，看得见的
The visual ability of cats is much better than that of dogs at night.
晚上猫的视觉比狗的要好得多。

barrier
n.1. sth. used to keep people or things apart or to prevent or control their movement
栅栏，关卡，检票口
Because the road was being repaired, barriers were put up to prevent people from driving in that area.
因为修路而架起了栅栏，不让汽车开到那里。
2. (to) sth. non-physical that keeps people apart or prevents movement
障碍，隔阂
Mary did not let her lack of money become a barrier to getting a good education.
玛丽不让缺钱成为她接受良好教育的障碍。
屏障

addition
n.1. the act of adding
加，加法
The teacher taught the pupils to do addition.
老师教小学生做加法。
2. sth. or sb. added
增加的人（或物）
He will be an important addition to our board.
他将是新加入我们董事会的重要人物。

reflect
v.1. think quietly and deeply
深思，考虑，反省
You should reflect on this problem before you make a decision.
你得考虑一下这个问题再作决定。
2. show
反映, 体现
Her poor grades reflect her lack of interest in school.
她成绩很差，说明她在学校对读书没有兴趣。
3. turn, throw or bend back sth. from a surface
反射，显示
The sky and the brightly colored trees were reflected in the calm surface of the lake.
平静的湖面上映出了天空和色彩艳丽的树木的倒影。

environment
n.the physical and social conditions in which people live
环境，周围状况，自然环境
We should take care not to hurt our environment.
我们得小心不要破坏环境。

critical
a.1. of or being in a moment of great danger, difficulty or uncertainty
决定性的，关键性的，危急的
It is reported that the meeting is critical for future decisions.
据报道这次会议对于以后的决定是至关重要的。
2. giving a careful judgment of the qualities of sth.
批评的，批判的
The boss was critical of his men who were slow and careless.
老板对于干活又慢又粗心的人很挑剔。

ability
n.the fact of having the skill or knowledge of doing sth.
能力，本领, 才能，才智
People believe that many abilities are developed through practice.
人们相信人的许多能力是从实践中获得的。

perspective
n.a way of considering sth.
视角，观点，想法
The new discovery put an entirely different perspective on the case.
新的发现使我们要从完全不同的角度来看待这个案子。

peer
n.a person of the same age, class or position
同龄人，同等地位的人
The child is much cleverer than his peers.
这孩子比他同龄人聪明得多。

instruct
vt.1. give orders or directions to
指示，命令；通知
Mr. Smith instructed his men that they only got two days to finish all the work.
史密斯先生通知手下，他们只有两天时间来完成全部任务。
2. give knowledge or information to
教育，指导
Who is instructing your typing?
谁在教你打字？

instructor
n.a teacher
教师；指导者

arise
vi.1. come into being, appear, happen
产生；出现；发生
Birds also attack the crops when the opportunity arises.
一有机会鸟也会来吃庄稼。
2. (from) come from
（由... ...）引起，（由... ...）产生，起源于
Mistakes often arise from misunderstandings.
错误常常源于误会。
3. get up from sitting or sleeping
起身，起床
He arose at 6:30 a.m. as usual.
像平时一样，他6点半起床。

absolute
a.complete, perfect
十足的，地道的
If I were president I must be given absolute power.
如果我是总统，我必须拥有绝对的权力。

absolutely
ad.completely, perfectly
十足地，地道
She is absolutely sure that she can do the job.
她非常确信自己能做这项工作。

understanding
n.1. the ability to know and learn
理解力
The child has no understanding problems according to the doctor.
据医生说，那小孩没有理解能力方面的问题。
2. sympathetic and therefore not blaming
谅解
The two sides have reached an understanding.
双方达成了一项谅解。

reinforce
vt.add strength or support to make (some idea, etc.) stronger or firmer
增强，加强，加固
The scientist reinforced his viewpoints by giving many facts.
科学家列举出许多事实来加强他的观点。

requirement
n.sth. that is needed or necessary
要求，必要条件; 需要，需要的东西
Students who cannot meet the minimum requirements would not be able to pass the exams.
不能达到最低要求的学生将不可能通过考试。

likely
a.that can be reasonably expected, probably
可能的，有希望的
It is not likely that he would come at this time of the day.
他不太可能这个时候来。
ad.probably
可能地

commit
vt.1. decide to use ... for a particular purpose
使用; 用于
The government committed millions of dollars to develop online learning.
政府把几百万美金用于发展网络教育。
2. do sth. wrong or illegal
犯（错误、罪行等），干（蠢事、坏事等)
This is the man who committed the murder.
这就是犯杀人罪的那个人。
3. (oneself)promise to a certain cause
使承担义务，使承诺
The government has committed itself to improving education.
政府承诺将发展教育。

input
n.[U]information or some other kinds of resources that are put in/into
输入，投入
Any information that you put into a computer is taken as input.
输进计算机的任何信息均被视为输入。

essential
a.completely necessary
必不可少的，绝对必要的，非常重要的
It is essential to remember every detail.
记住每个细节至关重要。
n.(usu. pl.) sth. necessary
要素，要点
What are the essentials of good teaching?
成功教学的要素是什么？

challenge
vt.1. question the rightness of
对... ... 质疑，对... ... 怀疑
They challenged the authority of what he said.
他们对他所说话的权威性提出质疑。
2. invite sb. to compete against one in
向... ... 挑战
To prove that he was faster than his friend, John challenged him to a race.
为了证明他比他的朋友跑得快，约翰提出与他赛跑。

normally
ad.in the usual way or to the usual degree
通常，正常地
a normally active child
活泼正常的孩子

sequence
n.1. 连续，一连串
a sequence of good news
一连串的好消息
2. 次序，顺序
having the names in alphabetical sequence
把名字按字母顺序排列

sequential
a.of, forming or following in sequence
连续的

vital
a.1.very important
极其重要的
It is vital for the government to take immediate measures to deal with the situation.
政府立即采取措施对付这种局势是极其重要的。
2. full of life and force
有生命的，充满生机的

effective
a.1. producing the desired result
有效的，生效的，起作用的
Their efforts to improve the school have been very effective.
他们改善学校环境的努力卓有成效。
2. having a pleasing effect
效果好的, 给人印象深刻的

play a role in

Language Points

Language Point 1
While regular schools still exist, the virtual classroom plays an important role in today's learning community. (Para.1)
Meaning: Though there are regular schools, the virtual classroom has a very important position and job in today's education.
play a role in: have a position or job in...
People have come to understand that computer is playing an increasingly important role in our life. 人们开始意识到计算机在我们生活中起着越来越重要的作用。
  community: n. a group of people living together and/or united by shared interests
  the academic community 学术界
  the black community 黑人社区

Language Point 2
Job opportunities for students are expanding rapidly and more people of all ages are becoming aware of online learning that allows them to study at home. (Para.1)
Meaning: Students have many more chances to get jobs, and more people of different ages are beginning to learn about online learning which allows them to study at home.
expand: v. (cause to) grow larger 
Iron expands when it is heated. 加热时铁会膨胀。
The company has expanded its operations in America by building a factory there. 这家公司在美国建立了一家工厂，从而扩大了在美业务。
people of all ages: people of different ages
be aware of: having knowledge or understanding of
I'm quite aware of how you feel about this problem. 我很清楚你对这个问题的感受。
He was so deep in his reading that he wasn't aware his father had entered. 他读书很专心，连父亲进门都不知道。

unique: a. unusual; being the only one of itself

Language Point 5
This forum for communication removes the visual barriers that hinder some students from expressing themselves. (Para.3)
Meaning: As a place for communication, the online method doesn't require people to see each other personally. So some students won't feel it difficult to express their ideas.
visual: a. gained by or connected with seeing
Visual knowledge of a place of battle helps a general to plan his attack. 亲临战场对将军制定攻击计划很有帮助。
The visual arts are painting and dancing, etc. as opposed to music and literature. 视觉艺术是绘画，舞蹈等，与音乐和文学相对。
barrier: n. something placed in the way in order to prevent or control the movement of people or things   
The police put up barriers to control the crowd. 警察搭起路障对人群加以控制。
The color of one's skin shouldn't be a barrier to success. 肤色不应该成为成功的障碍。
hinder: vt. prevent sb. from doing sth.; prevent sth. from happening
I'm sorry that some business hindered my answering your letter. 很抱歉，由于忙于一些事务，未能回信。
Don't talk to me now; you will hinder me. 现在不要和我讲，那样会妨碍我的。

Language Point 6

Meaning: What is more, students are given time to think about the information carefully before replying.
reflect: v. 1) express, give an idea of

The book reflected the author's idea on marriage. 这本书反映了作者的婚姻观。
2) throw back (sound, heat, light or an image)
  The smooth surface of the lake reflected the light of the house. 平静如镜的湖面映射出房屋的灯光。

reflect on: think carefully
I have been reflecting on what you said these days. 这些天我一直在考虑你说的话。
You should reflect on the likely result before you decide to do something. 决定做一件事之前应考虑可能产生的后果。

Language Point 7
In the virtual classroom nearly all communication is written, so it is critical that students feel comfortable expressing themselves in writing. (Para.5)
Meaning: In the online classroom, people communicate with each other by writing, so it is very important that students do feel at ease in writing to express their opinions.
  critical: a. 1) very important; very serious or dangerous
  I didn't realize the unemployment problem was all that critical here. 我原来不知道这儿的失业问题这么严重。
  Note: In the that-clause of "it is/was critical that ..." pattern, we should either use "should + bare infinitive" or simply use "bare infinitive." Very often, fluent native speakers use only the 'bare infinitive.'
  It was critical that the teacher (should) not punish the students who gave incorrect answers. 关键的是那位老师不应该惩罚回答错误的学生。
  It is critical that you (should) keep up with the progress of an online course. 跟上网络课程的进度十分重要。
2) finding fault
  Don't be critical of him; he is a beginner. 不要对他挑剔, 他是初学者。
  She looks on everything with a critical eye. 她用批判的眼光观察每一件事。

Language Point 10
This means students must be absolutely explicit with their comments and requests. (Para.7)
Meaning: This means students must express their ideas and requests very clearly.
absolutely: adv. completely
It is difficult to cross the desert by car, but not absolutely impossible. 开车穿越沙漠很难，但并非完全不可能。
They seemed to have absolutely no sense of moral principles. 他们似乎完全没有道德原则。
explicit: a. clear and fully expressed
He gave an explicit description of how the accident happened. 他清楚地讲述了事故发生的经过。
The agreement is explicit in saying the rent must be paid by the tenth of every month. 这份协议清楚地说明了必须在每个月十号付房租。
comment: n. opinion about an event or a person 
She asked the Minister if he had any comment on the government's new decision. 她问部长对政府的新决定有何评论。
There has been no comment so far from the police about the case. 到目前为止警方对这一案件没有任何评论。

Language Point 11
If they experience technical difficulties, or problems in understanding something about the course, they MUST speak up; (Para.7)
Meaning: If they have equipment problems or have problems in understanding the course content or instructions, they must tell others.
Please pay attention to the word "experience" as this word is used as a noun with different meanings as well as a verb.
experience: something that happens to one and has an effect on the mind and feelings
(Note: "Experience" used in this sense is a countable noun; we can say: "experiences" or "an experience".)
Learning a foreign language was one of the most difficult yet most rewarding experiences of my life. (Text A) 学习外语是我一生中最艰苦也是最有意义的经历。
But when "experience" is used in the sense of "knowledge or skill, which comes from practice in an activity" (经验，感受，体验), it is an uncountable noun. 
Don't correct him all the time; he'll learn by experience. 别总是纠正他，他会从经验中学习的。
And "experience" can be used as a verb, too, meaning, "feel, suffer, or learn by an experience" .
Margaret Rudland, head teacher in Hammersmith, also thinks children must experience actual peer relations. (Text C) 海默斯密斯学校校长玛格利特.鲁兰也认为孩子们必须与同龄人交往。
