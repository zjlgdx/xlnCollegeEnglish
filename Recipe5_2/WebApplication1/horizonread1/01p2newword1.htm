1. off and on
from time to time; sometimes 断断续续地;有时
  e.g. It has been raining off and on for a week.

 

  

2. possibility
n. 可能(性)
  e.g. Is there any possibility of your getting to London this week?

 

3. take hold
become established 生根，确立
  e.g. The newly planted vines quickly took hold.

 

  

4. bore
vt. make (sb. ) become tired and lose interest 使(人)厌烦
  e.g. I hope you're not getting bored listening to me.

 

5. associate
vt. join or connect together; bring in the mind 使联系起来;使联想
  e.g. Through science we've got the idea of associating progress with future.

 

  

6. assignment
n. a piece of work that is given to a particular person (分配的)工作，任务，作业
  e.g. I hardly ever take pictures except on assignment.

 

7. turn out
produce 编写;生产，制造
  e.g. The company turns out new computer games at a great rate.

 

  

8. agony
n. very great pain or suffering of mind or body (身心的)极度痛苦
  e.g. The wounded man was in agony.

 

9. assign
vt. give as a share or duty 分配，分派
  e.g. Those rooms have been assigned to us.

 

  

10. anticipate
vt. expect 预期，期望
  e.g. The police had anticipated trouble from the soccer fans and were at the ground in large numbers.

 

11. tedious
a. boring and lasting for a long time 乏味的;冗长的
  e.g. The tedious play put the audience to sleep.

 

  

12. reputation
n. 名声;名誉
  e.g. He is a man of good reputation.

 

13. inability
n. lack of power, skill or ability 无能，无力
  e.g. Her inability to concentrate could cause an accident.

 

  

14. inspire
vt. fill (sb.) with confidence, eagerness, etc. 激励，鼓舞
  e.g. The last leaf on the tree that never fell off inspired the dying patient with the will to live on.

 

15. formal
a. (too) serious and careful in manner and behavior; based on correct or accepted rules 刻板的，拘谨的;正式的，正规的
  e.g. Business letters are usually formal, but we write in an informal way to family members or friends.

 

  

16. rigid
a. (often disapproving) fixed in behavior, views or methods; strict 一成不变的;严格的
  e.g. My father is very rigid in his thinking.

 

17. hopelessly
ad. very much; without hope 十分，极度;绝望地
  e.g. The story is hopelessly confusing.

 

  

18. out of date
old-fashioned 过时的
  e.g. Will denim jeans ever go out of date?

 

19. prim
a. (usu. disapproving) (of a person) too formal or correct in behavior and showing a dislike of anything rude; neat 古板的，拘谨的;循规蹈矩的;整洁的
  e.g. Those young ladies are much too prim and proper to enjoy such a rude joke.

 

  

20. primly
ad. 古板地，拘谨地;循规蹈矩地;整洁地
  e.g. When the boss came in, all the workers were sitting there primly.

 

 

21. severe
a. completely plain; causing very great pain, difficulty, worry, etc. 朴素的;严重的，剧烈的
  e.g. Shortages of staff are very severe in some places.

 

  

22. necktie
n. tie 领带
  e.g. He likes to ware narrow neckties.

 

23. jaw
n. 颌，颚
  e.g. Which jaw moves up and down when you talk?

 

  

24. comic
a. 滑稽的;喜剧的
n. 连环漫画(册)
  e.g. Most of these trips had exciting or comic moments.

 

25. antique
n. 古物,古玩
  e.g. He can tell a real antique from a fake one.

 

  

26. tackle
vt. try to deal with 处理，应付
  e.g. I don't know how to tackle this problem.

 

27. essay
n. 散文，小品文;论说文
  e.g. The other day I came across an interesting essay on the war with Napoleon.

 

  

28. distribute
vt. divide and give out among people, places, etc. 分发，分配，分送
  e.g. In the move most of the furniture was left to the neighbors or distributed among friends.

 

29. finally
ad. at last 最终，终于
  e.g. Finally, I should like to thank you all for coming and to wish you a pleasant journey home.

 

  

30. face up to
be brave enough to accept or deal with 勇敢地接受或对付
  e.g. She must face up to the fact that she is no longer young.

 

31. scan
v. look through quickly 浏览，粗略地看
  e.g. He scanned the newspaper while having his breakfast.

 

  

32. spaghetti
n. 意大利式细面条
  e.g. Many people consider spaghetti as their favored food.

 

33. title
n. a name given to a book, film, etc. 标题，题目
vt. give a name to 给…加标题，加题目于
  e.g. The book was first published in 1969 under the title of "Life and Death".
 

 

  

34. extraordinary
a. very unusual or strange 不同寻常的;奇特的
  e.g. He is a man of extraordinary talents.

 

 

35. sequence
n. 一连串相关的事物; 次序，顺序
  e.g. A sequence of bad harvests forced some African countries to ask for foreign aid.

 

  

36. image
n. a picture formed in the mind 形象; 印象;(图)像
  e.g. I had this image of London in my head which was totally different from how it really is.

 

37. adult
n. a fully grown person or animal 成年人;成年动物
  e.g. The adults in the family like the world news section.

 

  

38. humor
n. 心情;幽默，诙谐
  e.g. He is in a good humor today.

 

39. recall
vt. bring back to the mind; remember 回想起，回忆起
  e.g. I don't recall his name or where I met him.

 

  

40. argument
n. 论据，论点;争论
  e.g. There are many arguments against smoking.

 

41. respectable
a. (of behavior, appearance, etc.) socially acceptable 可敬的;体面的;文雅的
  e.g. Are these clothes respectable enough for Mrs Whitehouse's party?

 

  

42. put down
write down 写下
  e.g. Here's my address -- put it down before you forget it.

 

 

43. recapture
vt. (lit) bring back into the mind; experience again 再现;再次经历
  e.g. They took a second honeymoon and tried to recapture the their earlier happiness.

 

  

44. relive
vt. experience again, esp. in one's imagination 再体验，重温
  e.g. That was an experience I should not like to relive.

 

45. violate
vt. act against 违背，违反
  e.g. They violated the ceasefire（停火）agreement.

 

  

46. compose
vt. write or create (music, poetry, etc.) 创作
  e.g. He teaches music and also composes music.
 

 

47. turn in
hand in (work that one has done) 交(作业)
  e.g. For your final grade, each of you must turn in a 7-page paper.

 

  

48. command
n. , v. 命令，指令
  e.g. The officer commanded his men to fire.

 

49. discipline
n. punishment; order kept (among school-children, soldiers, etc.) 惩罚，处分;纪律
  e.g. The army was criticized for its harsh discipline.

 

  

50. what's more
in addition, more importantly 而且，此外;更有甚者
  e.g. How can you love this man? He watches TV all day long, and what's more, he seems not to have brushed his teeth for months!

 

51. contempt
n. 轻视，轻蔑
  e.g. Many voters hold their politicians in contempt.

 

  

52. ridicule
n. making or being made fun of 嘲笑，嘲弄;被戏弄
  e.g. I know that ridicule may be a shield, but it is not a weapon.

 

53. open-hearted
a. sincere, frank 诚挚的
  e.g. He was open-hearted in his wish to help us.

 

  

54. hold back
prevent the expression of (feelings, tears, etc.) 控制(感情、眼泪等)
  e.g. She kept trying to hold back her tears.

 

55. avoid
vt. keep or get away from 避免
  e.g. The pilots had to take emergency action to avoid a disaster.

 

  

56. demonstration
n. act of showing or proving sth. 表明;证明
  e.g. Can you give us a simple demonstration of gravity?

 

57. career
n. 生涯，事业;职业
  e.g. We can learn much by reading about the careers of great men.

 

  

58. seal
n. 印，图章
  e.g. Please put a seal on the box.

 

59. essence
n. the most important quality of a thing 本质;精髓
  e.g. Accuracy is one of the essence of the English language.

 

  

60. congratulation
n. (usu.pl) expression of joy for sb.'s success, luck, etc. 祝贺，恭喜
  e.g. We offer our congratulations on her success.
