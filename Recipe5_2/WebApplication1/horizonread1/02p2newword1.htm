1. cabbie
n. (infml) a taxi driver
  e.g. Cabbie, how far is it to Johnson Street?

 

  

2. be lost in/lose oneself in
be absorbed in, be fully occupied with 专心致志于
  e.g. He was lost in thought, staring at the book in front of him.

 

3. windshield
n. (AmE) the glass window in the front of a car, truck, etc. (汽车的)挡风玻璃
  e.g. Since I couldn't find my friend, I wrote him a note and stuck it on the windshield of his car.

 

  

4. cab
n. a taxi 出租车
  e.g. The cabs were lined up outside the station.

 

 

5. available
a. able to be used, had or reached 可用的;可得到的
  e.g. Details of the competition are available from our head office.

 

 

  

6. apologetically
ad. showing or saying that one is sorry for some fault or wrong 道歉地,带着歉意地
  e.g. Finally the boy's parents appeared and gave him a hug apologetically.

 

7. or something
(infml) (used when you are not very sure about what you have just said) 诸如此类的事
  e.g. Here's some money. Get yourself a sandwich or something.

 

  

8. go ahead
continue; begin
  e.g. "Could I ask you a rather personal question?" "Of course, go ahead."

 

9. know/learn by heart
memorize, remember exactly 记住，能背出
  e.g. The pupils were required to learn the poems by heart.

 

  

10. estimate
vt. form a judgement about 估计
  e.g. We haven't estimated the proper price for the contract yet.

 

11. might/may (just) as well
不妨,(也)无妨
  e.g. The weather was so bad we might (just) as well have stayed at home.

  

12. not much of a
not a good 不太好的
  e.g. He is not much of a father, but he is an outstanding professor.  

13. keep up
continue without stopping 保持
  e.g. They risk losing their homes because they can no longer keep up the repayments.  

 

  

14. correspondence
n. the act of writing, receiving or sending letters; letters 通信(联系);信件
  e.g. His interest in writing came from a long correspondence with a close friend.

 

15. practically
ad. almost
  e.g. The holidays are practically over; there's only one day left.

 

  

16. kid
n. (infml) a child
  e.g. I am taking the kids (= my children) to the zoo this afternoon.

 

17. all the way
自始至终，一直
  e.g. I am with you all the way.

 

  

18. neighborhood
n. 街坊;四邻
  e.g. She is well-known in the neighborhood for her charitable（慈善的）work.  

 

19. kind/sort of
(infml) a little bit, in some way or degree 有几分,有点儿
  e.g. It was kind of odd that he didn't come.  

 

  

20. lose touch
失去联系
  e.g. I am trying to get in touch with my brother; he emigrated to Australia, and I lost touch with him.

 

21. a couple of
几个;一对，一双
  e.g. Across the street there are a couple of police officers standing guard.

 

  

22. guy
n. 家伙;伙计
  e.g. He's quite a nice guy when you get to know him.

 

23. tough
a. (infml) unfortunate; difficult; strong 不幸的;困难的;坚固的;坚强的
  e.g. The government took tough measures to limit the money supply.

 

  

24. on one's mind
挂记在心头
  e.g. She looks very worried; I wonder what is on her mind.

 

25. keep in touch (with)
(与...)保持联系，保持接触
  e.g. I haven't really kept in touch with people I knew at school.

 

  

26. come up
happen, occur , esp. unexpectedly (尤指意想不到地)发生，出现
  e.g. I'll let you know if anything comes up.

 

27. shrug
v. lift (the shoulders) slightly 耸(肩)
  e.g. She just shrugged (her shoulders), saying she didn't know and didn't care.

 

  

28. urge
v. try very hard to persuade 力劝，催促
  e.g. They urged us to give our support.

 

29. postpone
vt. delay 推迟，使延期
  e.g. We're postponing our holiday until August.

 

  

30. reference
n. 提及，谈到;参考，查阅
  e.g. Use this dictionary for easy reference.

 

31. absolutely
ad. completely 完全地,极其
absolute a.
  e.g. I'm absolutely starving (= very hungry).

 

  

32. reunion
n. (家人、朋友、同事等久别后的)重聚
  e.g. We hold an annual reunion of former students of the college.

 

33. go by
(of time) pass (时间)逝去
  e.g. Two years went by.

 

  

34. hang out
(infml) stay in or near a place, not doing very much 闲荡;徘徊
  e.g. They normally hang out in the pub.

 

35. every now and then
sometimes, at times
  e.g. I write to him every now and then.

 

  

36. mostly
ad. almost all; generally 几乎全部; 多半，大体
  e.g. She uses her car mostly for driving to work.

 

37. awful
a.(infml) (used to add force) very great; very bad or unpleasant 非常的, 极大的; 可怕的，糟糕的
  e.g. It was awful to see him in such pain.

 

  

38. choke up
become too upset to speak (因激动等)哽得说不出话;堵塞
choke v. (使)窒息;堵塞
  e.g. Losing my job left me completely choked up; I was so upset that I didn't know what to say.

 

39. destination
n. 目的地
  e.g. The parcel was sent to the wrong destination.

 

  

40. skip
v. 略过,跳过;跳跃
  e.g. The speaker kept skipping from one subject to another.

41. sorrowful
a. showing or causing sadness 伤心的，悲伤的
sorrow n.
  e.g. We all share your sorrow over this sad loss.

 

  

42. in the distance
far away
  e.g. You can see the ancient ruins in the distance.

 


43. unpack
v. take out (things) from (a suitcase, etc.) 打开
  e.g. Have you unpacked (your clothes/ your suitcase) yet?

 

  

44. right away
at once
  e.g. He wants to see you right away.
