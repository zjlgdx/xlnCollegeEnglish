seemingly
ad. in a way that appears to be true but may in fact not be 似乎，表面上地
  e.g. He remains confident and seemingly untroubled by our recent problems.

  

shallow
a. not deep 浅的
  e.g. The water is quite shallow for some distance.

 

eternal
a. continuing forever 永恒的
  e.g. Rome has been called the Eternal City.

 

  

overtake
vt. (overtook, overtaken) catch up to and pass 赶上，超过
  e.g. After only two years in the American market, our US sales have now overtaken our sales in Europe.

 

imminent
a. likely to happen soon 即将来临的，逼近的
  e.g. According to the weather report a rainstorm is imminent.

 

  

imitation
n. an act or instance of imitating 模仿，效法
  e.g. She can do a wonderful imitation of a blackbird's song.

 

vivid
a. producing a strong or clear impression on the senses 生动的，清晰的
  e.g. She gave the police a vivid description of the accident.

 

   pump
vt. （用泵）抽
  e.g. The villagers had pumped the well dry, and could get no more water.

 

productive
a. producing goods, crops, or wealth 生产的，多产的
  e.g. They work hard, but their efforts are not very productive.

 

  

come upon
find by accident; come across 偶然发现；突然遇见
  e.g. I came upon this book in the attic. Would you like it?

 

at a/one stroke
by a single action 一举，一下子
  e.g. They threatened to cancel the whole project at one stroke.

 

  

exotic
a. from another part of the world; foreign 外来的；外国的
  e.g. There's an exhibition of exotic houseplants.  

 

infinite
a. limitless, never-ending 无限的，无穷的
  e.g. Listening to the late quartets on that little gramophone, I experienced the most infinite musical joy that I had ever known.  

 

 

repetition
n. doing or saying the same thing again 重复
  e.g. I want no repetition of your bad behavior.

 

be given to
be in the habit of doing 习惯
  e.g. He's given to taking long walks.

 

   shrink
(shrank, shrunk) v. make or become smaller （使）收缩，缩短
  e.g. Your sweater will shrink if you wash it at too high a temperature.

 

summon
vt. call or send for 召唤，传唤
  e.g. He summoned up a smile, though it seemed to take all his strength.

 

  

run over
hit and crush under a vehicle （车等）碾过
  e.g. She was run over and killed by a train.

 

monument
n. a building, etc. built in memory of a person or historical event 纪念碑
  e.g. This pillar is a monument to all those who died in the civil war.
