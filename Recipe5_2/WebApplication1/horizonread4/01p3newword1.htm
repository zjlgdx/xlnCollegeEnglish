obstacle
n. a thing that blocks one's way 障碍物
  e.g. Her father's opposition remained their only obstacle.

  

in case
so as to be safe if (sth.) happens 万一；假使
  e.g. Take a hat with you in case the sun is very hot.

 

favourable
a. helpful; showing approval 有利的；赞同的
  e.g. This region has a favourable climate for growing apples.

 

  

hold out
offer; last; stand fast 提出，提供；维持；坚持
  e.g. The forthcoming talks hold out the hope of real arms reductions.

 

due to
because of 由于
  e.g. He was late due to the very heavy traffic.

 

  

cancel
vt. decide that (sth. arranged in advance) will not happen 取消
  e.g. The match had to be cancelled because of bad weather.

 

call off
cancel 取消
  e.g. They have called off their trip to Tibet.

 

   on the eve of
immediately before 在…前夕
  e.g. We are going to have a family reunion on the eve of the New Year.

 

absent
a. not present; missing 缺席的；没有的
  e.g. He was absent from the meeting.

 

  

stiff
a. (of a wind, etc.) blowing strongly; not easily bent, changed in shape, etc.（风等）强烈的；硬的；僵直的
  e.g. He walk forward difficultly because of the stiff wind.

 

complication
n. a thing that makes a situation more complex or difficult 复杂情况；（新出现的）困难；障碍
  e.g. Another complication was the excess of imports over exports.

 

  

withstand
vt. endure without giving in; resist 承受；抵挡
  e.g. This building is able to withstand destruction forces of nature.  

 

concern
vt. worry; be relevant to; affect 使担心；涉及；影响
concerned a. worried; troubled 担心的；忧虑的
  e.g. We're rather concerned about father's health.  

 

 

prior to
before 在…之前
  e.g. We received no notification prior to today's date.

 

pin down
prevent from moving 把…困住; 使不能行动
  e.g. He was pinned down by his attackers.

 

   objective
n. a thing aimed at or wished for 目标
  e.g. My objective this summer will be learning to swim.

 

responsible
a. being the cause of; legally or morally obliged; reliable and sensible 作为原由的； 应承担责任的；负责可靠的
  e.g. The bus driver is responsible for the passengers safety.

 

  

contest
v. fight for; struggle to win or keep 争夺
n. 争夺；竞争；比赛
  e.g. How many people are contesting the seat on the council?

 

pay off
bring good results; succeed 取得好结果；成功
  e.g. His hard work paid off when he got a promotion.

 

  

boast
v. talk about in a manner showing too much pride and satisfaction 吹嘘
  e.g. The boy boasted that his bicycle was of the best quality of all the bicycles in the school.
