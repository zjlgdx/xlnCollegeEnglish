haste
n. quickness of movement; hurry 急忙；匆忙
  e.g. She made haste to tell her mother the good news.

  

characterize
vt. be typical of (sb./sth.); be characteristic of 成为…的特征；以…为特征
  e.g. Bright colors and bold strokes characterize his early paintings.

 

on the wing
in motion; in flight 正在进行中；在飞行中
  e.g. He likes to photograph birds on the wing.

 

  

under pressure
influenced by urgency or compulsion; suffering stress 被催逼的；承受压力的
  e.g. She is constantly under pressure and it is affecting her health.

 

volume
n. （书）卷，册；体积；分量
  e.g. Volume 2 of Shaw's Complete Works is missing.

 

  

signify
vt. be a sign of (sth.); mean 表示…的意思；意味
  e.g. He signified his content with a nod.

 

thrill
v. （使）非常兴奋，（使）非常激动
  e.g. We thrilled at the good news.

 

   browse
vi. read or glance through information carried on computer networks; examine books in a casual way （网上）浏览；翻阅书刊
  e.g. He often browses through newspapers during lunch.

 

caption
vt. 给…加标题（或说明文字等）；为…配字幕

 

  

waterproof
a. that cannot be penetrated by water 不透水的；防水的
  e.g. This cloth is waterproof.

 

forth
ad. 向前方，向前
  e.g. The explorers ventured forth to discover new land.

 

  

parallel
a. having the same distance between each other at every point 平行的
  e.g. The railway line runs parallel with the highway.  

 

opt
vi. decide to do sth.; choose 选择；作出抉择
  e.g. They opt for more holiday instead of more pay.  

 

 

buzz
n. noisy activity; agitation 喧嚣繁忙；激动不安
  e.g. the buzz of voices in the crowded room

 

endurance
n. state or power of enduring 忍耐力
  e.g. The marathon tests a runner's endurance.

 

   confess
v. acknowledge, often reluctantly; say or admit (that one has committed a crime, etc.) 承认；坦白，供认
  e.g. Mr. Foster confessed that he'd broken the speed limit.

 

no wonder
it is not surprising that 难怪
  e.g. Under such circumstances, it is no wonder that he experiences difficulties.

 

  

run the risk of/ a risk
do sth. that exposes one to a danger, possibility, etc. 冒（…）危险
  e.g. If we don't take more money with us, we run the risk of running out over the weekend.

 

suspend
vt. prevent sth. from being in effect for a time; hang (sth.) up 暂停，中止；悬，挂
  e.g. We suspended the building work during the rain.

 

  

zone
n. area or region with a particular feature 区域；范围；地带
  e.g. a special economic zone
