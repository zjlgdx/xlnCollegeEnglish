Language Points

Language Point 1
…determined to strike out on independent paths. (Para. 1)
Meaning: …determined to make an independent effort to be on their own.

Language Point 2
…determined to strike out on independent paths. (Para. 1)
strike out: start being independent
I knew it was time I struck out on my own.我知道是自己独立的时候了。
John quit his job and struck out as a traveling salesman. 约翰辞去了原来的工作，开始做巡回推销员。

Language Point 3
Although the 18-year-old sisters had requested rooms in different dorms,…(Para. 1)
dorm: n. an informal word, meaning the same as "dormitory" — a large room or building containing many beds, esp. in a boarding school or university

Language Point 4
While Katie got along with her roommate, Sarah was miserable. (Para. 1)
get along (with): If two or more people get along, they like each other and are friendly to each other.
They seem to be getting along much better these days.他们这些天似乎相处得更好了。
I really don't get along with my sister's husband. 我真的和我姐夫相处不来。
Do you get along with your neighbour all right? 你和你的邻居相处得还好吗？

Language Point 5
She and her roommate silently warred over matters ranging from when the lights should be turned off to how the furniture should be arranged. (Para. 1)
Meaning: She and her roommate fought secretly /without speaking out with each other about some small and unimportant matters such as when the lights should be turned off and how the furniture should be arranged.

Language Point 6
She and her roommate silently warred over matters ranging from when the lights should be turned off to how the furniture should be arranged. (Para. 1)
war over: argue or fight about sth., or about which side should possess (sth. or sb.)
They often warred over who should fetch the water. 他们经常就谁该去打水而争吵。
The natives have been warring over this small piece of land without either side winning. 当地人一直在为这一小块地争斗，双方谁都没有赢。
The saddest part about a broken marriage is the way the parents so often war over the children. 破裂的婚姻最不幸的一面是父母双方经常就子女的归属争吵不休。

Language Point 7
…matters ranging from when the lights should be turned off to how the furniture should be arranged. (Para. 1)
range from… to: vary from (sth. smaller or less) to (sth. bigger or more)
Dress sizes range from petite to extra large. 服装尺码从特小号到特大号不等。
Prices range from a few dollars to a thousand dollars. 价格从几美元到1,000美元不等。

Language Point 8
Finally, they divided the room in two and gave up on oral communication, communicating primarily through short notes. (Para. 1)
Meaning: At last, they divide the room in two and lost the desire to talk to each other and their communication was chiefly through short notes.

Language Point 9
…and gave up on oral communication, communicating primarily through short notes. (Para. 1)
