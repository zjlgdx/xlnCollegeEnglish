contrary
n., a. opposite 反面（的）；相反（的）
  e.g. Cruelty is the contrary of kindness.

  

memorial
a. serving to keep in mind 纪念（性）的
n. sth. done or made to remind people of an event, person, etc. 纪念物；纪念碑；纪念仪式
  e.g. A memorial service is being held for her in St Paul's Church.

 

dwell
vi. (dwelt or dwelled) reside 居住
  e.g. American Indians used to dwell in the forests.

 

  

mysterious
a. full of, covered in, mystery 神秘的
  e.g. They're being very mysterious about their holiday plans.

 

live on
continue to live or exist 继续生活；继续存在
  e.g. She lived on for many years after her husband died.

 

  

shortly after
soon after …后不久
  e.g. They moved to Europe shortly after that.

 

lead to
cause 导致
  e.g. His mistakes led to his failure.

 

   applaud
v. show approval of by clapping the hands; praise, approve 鼓掌赞许；称赞
  e.g. The residents in the neighborhood all applauded the council's decision to close the small dye factory.

 

blur
v. make or become unclear（使）变模糊
  e.g. His eyes were blurred with tears.

 

  

entity
n. sth. that has real existence; a thing's existence 实际存在物；实体
  e.g. Persons and corporations are equivalent entities under the law.

 

presumably
ad. supposedly 可能，大概，据推测
  e.g. Presumably the bad weather has delayed the flight.

 

  

what of
what about …怎么样
  e.g. Now we've talked about work on tulips, what of other commercially important flowers, like roses?  

 

dwarf
vt. cause to appear small by contrast or distance 使显得渺小，使相形见绌   e.g. His children were dwarfed by insufficient food.  

 

 

in exile
away from one's country or home, esp. as a punishment 于流放中

 

originate
v. have as a cause or beginning; be the creator of 起源于，来自
  e.g. The use of steam originated many other reforms.

 

   derive
v. get; trace (sth. from a source) 获得；追溯…的起源
  e.g. Many English words are derived from Latin.

 

category
n. class or group of things in a complete system of grouping 种类；范畴   e.g. There are different categories of books in the library.

 

  

misery
n. state of being miserable; great suffering 悲惨的境遇；苦难，不幸
  e.g. lead a life of misery

 

ethic
n. system of moral principles, rules of conduct 道德标准，行为准则
  e.g. Such an action is a violation of medical ethics.

 

  

streak
n. an element of a specified kind in sb.'s character 个性特征
  e.g. There is a streak of cruelty in his character.
