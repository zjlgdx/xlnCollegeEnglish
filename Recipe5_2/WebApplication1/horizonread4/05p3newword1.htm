mercy
n. 仁慈；宽恕；宽容
  e.g. They showed little mercy to the enemies.

  

handicapped
a. suffering from a serious physical or mental disability 有生理缺陷的，残疾的
  e.g. Though handicapped by poor health, he persisted in working at the research project.

 

for life
for the rest of a person's life 终生
  e.g. As a result of the accident, he was disabled for life.

 

  

take (one's) revenge (on sb.)
return an injury 报仇，报复
revenge n. 报仇，报复
  e.g. They swore to take their revenge on the kidnappers.

 

good and ...
(infml) completely 完全，彻底
  e.g. I won't go until I'm good and ready.

 

  

depend on/upon
be decided by; need the support of (sb. or sth.) 取决于；依靠
  e.g. This area depends on the mining industry.

 

scoop
v. gather in or up as if with a scoop; take up or out with a scoop 掬起；用勺舀
  e.g. She scooped flour out of the bag.

 

  

freeze up
(cause sth. to) become completely covered or blocked with ice （使）结冰；（使）冻结
  e.g. The window has frozen up and I can't open it.

shove
v. go away; push roughly 走开；乱推，挤
  e.g. You aren't wanted here, so shove off!

 

  

self-conscious
a. uncomfortably and unnaturally aware of oneself and one's actions 忸怩的，不自然的
  e.g. She is self-conscious about her appearance.

 

slug
vt. hit (sb.) hard, esp. with the fist （用拳头）重击
  e.g. He felt as if he had been slugged by a piece of lead pipe.

 

  

gouge
v. push one's thumb into the eye of; remove (sth.) from a hole using a pointed object or one's fingers 用拇指挖出…的眼睛；挖，掘  

 

bank up
heap or pile in a bank; rise in or form a bank 将…堆积起来；聚集成堆
  e.g. They banked up the logs. 

 

 

let go
stop holding (sth.) 松手放开
  e.g. Will they let the hostages go?

 

stomp
v. strike or beat forcibly with the bottom of the foot; bring down (the foot) forcibly 重踩，重踏；跺（脚）

 

  

pat
vt. tap gently, usu. with the hand flat 轻拍，轻打
  e.g. I patted him on the shoulder.

put a stop to
stop (sth.) from happening any more; abolish 制止， 终止；废止
  e.g. She stood up and put a stop to all these rumors.

 

  

lucky
a. having or resulting from luck 侥幸的；幸运的
  e.g. I was lucky that I met you here.

 

adrift
a., ad. drifting; aimless 漂浮着（的）；漫无目的地（的）
  e.g. They were rescued after three days adrift on the sea.
