New Words

afterward(s)
ad.after the time mentioned; later
以后，后来
Let's go to the theatre first and eat afterwards.
我们先去看电影再去吃饭吧。
We had tea, and afterwards we sat in the garden for a while.
我们喝了茶，之后在花园里坐了一会儿。

quiz
v.ask sb. questions
询问，提问
She spent an hour being quizzed by journalists.
她花了一小时回答记者的提问。
n.1. a contest, esp. on television or radio, in which people try to answer questions to test their knowledge
（尤指电视、广播节目中的）问答比赛，智力竞赛
take part in a quiz
参加智力竞赛
a sports / music quiz
体育/音乐知识竞赛
2. a short informal test
小测验，考查
I often start the class with a short quiz to revise some vocabulary items.
我经常一上课就做一次复习词汇的小测验。

offense (英 offence)
n.1.[U] upset and hurt feelings or cause feelings of annoyance, often because sb. has been rude or shown a lack of respect
冒犯，伤感情
Do you think he took offence at what I said?
你认为他会对我说的话生气吗？
Don't be upset by what he said ─ he meant no offence.
你不要为他的话烦恼；他没有恶意。
2.[C] an illegal act; a crime
犯罪行为，罪行
a serious offence
一项重大的罪行
a minor offence
一项轻微的罪行
commit an offence against the law
犯法
Driving without a license is an offence.
无照驾驶是一种违法行为。

relevant
a.connected with what is happening or being discussed
有关的，相关的
The evidence is relevant to the case.
该证据与此案有关。
His color is not relevant to whether he's a good lawyer or not.
他的肤色与他是否是一个好律师无关。

irrelevant
a.not related to what is being discussed or considered and therefore of no importance
不相关的，不切题的，不重要的
Your question is interesting but irrelevant to our problem.
你提的问题很有趣，但和我们要处理的事情不相干。
His comment was really irrelevant to the discussion.
他的评论与讨论全然无关。

tide
n.1. [C] a direction in which opinion, events, luck, etc. seem to move
潮流，趋势
The tide of public opinion is turning against smoking.
公共舆论转向反对吸烟。
2. [C] a regular rise and fall in the level of the sea, caused by the attraction of the moon
潮，潮汐
at high / low tide
高/低潮位
Investigators are feeding weather and tide charts into a computer.
调查研究人员把天气情况和海潮变化图输入计算机。

transmission
n.1. [U] the action or process of transmitting or the state of being transmitted
传播，传送
the transmission of news / disease
消息的传达/疾病的传播
2. [C] a radio or television broadcast
广播或电视播送
a live transmission from Beijing
来自北京的现场直播
Millions would hear that transmission.
将有数百万人听到那次广播节目。

stadium
n.[C] a large enclosed area of land with rows of seats around and often with no roof, which is used for sporting or other entertainment events
体育场
Thousands of football fans packed into the stadium to watch the cup final.
成千上万的足球迷涌进了体育场看杯赛的决赛。

obligation
n.[C] a duty; a law, a promise, an influence, etc. that makes it necessary for sb. to do sth.
义务，责任

我没有时间帮他做事，事实上我自己有许多事要做。

无价值的，空洞的，虚伪的

他们的好意会变成现实还是成为空话？
2. having an empty space inside
中空的，空心的

investment
n.1. [U] a large amount of time, energy, emotion, etc. that one spends on sth.
（大量时间、精力或感情等的）投入

教这些孩子学英语也许是一种更好的时间投入。

overseas investment

guarantee
vt.promise with certainty

这只手表保修三年。

友谊的纽带/恋爱关系

联系两国的纽带

在家庭关系密切的社会中，人们往往长寿。

glue
n.[U] a sticky substance used for joining things together
胶，胶水

我想我们能用胶水来粘合那个杯子。

worship

崇敬, 敬仰, 敬慕

(对上帝或神灵)信奉，敬奉；拜神，敬神

他们定期做礼拜。

Language Points

Language Point 1
At dinner afterwards I was subjected to a hostile quizzing by a group of women in their thirties who claimed that my whole analysis ignored the most basic change of all. (Para. 1)
Meaning: At the following dinner I was questioned in an unfriendly manner by a group of women in their thirties, who stated that my whole analysis failed to consider the most basic aspect of family change.

Language Point 2
… I was subjected to a hostile quizzing ... (Para. 1)
subject ... to:

It is generally acknowledged to be true. 人们普遍认为这是对的。
2) publicly express thanks for help one has been given
I gratefully acknowledge the financial support from several local businesses. 我十分感谢几家地方企业所给予的财政资助。
We must not fail to acknowledge his services to the town. 我们必须感谢他对小镇所做的贡献。

Language Point 59
… some children are better off without … (Para. 10)
be better off (doing sth.): used to say that sb. is or would be happier or more satisfied if he / she were in a particular position or did a particular thing
She's better off without him. 没有他，她只会过得更好。
The weather was so bad we'd have been better off staying at home. 天气那么糟，我们待在家里就好了。

Language Point 60
… taking out their anger on everyone … (Para. 10)
take it / sth. out on sb.: behave in an unpleasant way towards sb. because one feels angry, disappointed, etc., although it is not his / her fault
OK, so you had a bad day. Don't take it out on me. 好吧，你今天过得糟透了，但不要拿我出气。
She tended to take her frustrations out on her family. 她往往把气发泄在家里人身上。

Language Point 61
… it results in poverty for many women and children … (Para. 11)
Meaning: … it (divorce) brings, as a result, poverty to many women and children …

Language Point 62
… and replaces it with one of regulated, arranged visits … (Para. 11)
regulate: v. control sth. by rules and restrictions
The policemen regulated traffic at the intersection. 警察在十字路口指挥交通。
The instrument regulates the temperature of the room. 这种仪器可控制室温。
Her mother strictly regulates how much TV she can watch. 她母亲严格控制她看电视的量。

Language Point 63
… it removes the father from the household, the only sensible basis for a working parental relationship; it places a terrible strain on the mother-child bond by saddling the mother with the double burden of playing the role of both mother and father. (Para. 11)
Meaning: … it (divorce) makes the father leave the family, and the only reasonable basis for him to support the children disappears; it creates a tense relationship between the mother and the child, as the mother is under the heavy pressure of performing the roles of both mother and father.

Language Point 64
… a terrible strain on the mother-child bond … (Para. 11)
strain: n. a state in which one is greatly troubled by anxieties and troubles; a state of difficulty, distrust, opposition, etc. between people
Their marriage is under great strain at the moment. 目前他们的婚姻承受着很大的压力。
The conflict placed further strain on relations between the two countries. 这次冲突使两国之间的关系进一步紧张。
Relax, and let us take the strain (= deal with the difficulties) for you. 放松些，让我们来为你分忧。
These constant delays are a strain on our patience. 如此一再延误使我们忍无可忍。

Language Point 65
… by saddling the mother with the double burden of playing … (Para. 11)
saddle sb. / oneself with sth.: (often passive) give sb. / oneself an unpleasant responsibility, task, debt, etc.
I've been saddled with organizing the conference. 我承担着组织会议的任务。
He was saddled with heavy debts. 他债台高筑。

Language Point 66
… to mold a child into a healthy member of the society. (Para. 11)
mold (BrE mould): v.
1) (into) strongly influence the way sb.'s character, opinions, etc. develop
The experience had moulded and coloured her whole life. 这次经历影响和丰富了她的整个人生。
He moulded them into a superb team. 他把他们打造成了一支一流球队。
2) (into) (from / out of / in) shape a soft substance into a particular form or object by pressing it or by putting it into a mould (模子)
First, mould the clay into the desired shape. 首先，把粘土捏成想要的形状。
The bust had been moulded in clay. 这座半身像是用粘土塑成的。

Language Point 67
… few lovers are willing to take on real responsibility for the welfare of another person's child. (Para. 11)
take on: accept a particular job or responsibility and begin to do what is needed
She took too much on and made herself ill. 她给自己太多负担，终于病倒了。
When I took this job on I didn't expect it to take all this time. 我接受这工作时没想到会花这么多时间。
With promotion, he had taken on greater responsibilities. 随着职务的提升，他担负的责任也更重了。

Language Point 68
… I would venture to suggest, … (Para. 12)
venture: v. (formal) say or do sth. in a careful way, esp. because it might upset or offend sb.
She hardly dared to venture an opinion. 她几乎不敢大胆发表看法。
I ventured to suggest that she might have made a mistake. 我冒昧指出她可能犯了一个错误。
I venture that you are behaving foolishly. 我冒昧说一句，你的做法很愚蠢。

Language Point 69
… make concessions for children … (Para. 12)
concession: n. sth. which is allowed or given up, in order to end an argument or to make a situation less difficult
The firm will be forced to make concessions if it wants to avoid a strike. 如果这个公司想避免罢工的话，它必须做出让步。
As a concession to the public outcry, the government reduced the tax. 作为对公众呼吁的让步，政府减少了税收。
concede: v. ( ~ sth. to sb. / ~ sb. sth.) admit sth. as true, logical, proper, etc., often unwillingly
He was forced to concede (that) there might be difficulties. 他被迫承认可能有困难。
He reluctantly conceded the point to me. / He reluctantly conceded me the point. 他勉强向我承认了这一点。

Language Point 70
And complete freedom will finally become our chain, leaving us with no choice but to live in loneliness, with no one who need to care about us. (Para. 12)
Meaning: And complete freedom will deny us all freedom in the end, making us have no choice but to stay lonely and receive no care from others.

Language Point 71
… leaving us with no choice but to live in loneliness, with no one who need to care about us. (Para. 12)
no choice but to: the only choice to (do sth.)
We have no choice but to close the hospital. 我们没有别的选择，只好关闭医院。
You leave me no choice but to give in. 你使我没有别的选择，只好让步。
He had no choice but to agree. 他别无选择，只好同意。
