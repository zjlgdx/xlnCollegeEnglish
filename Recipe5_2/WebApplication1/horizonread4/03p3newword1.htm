excerpt
n. a part quoted from a larger written or spoken work 选段，摘录
  e.g. I've seen a short excerpt from the film on television.

 

foundation
n. an organization that gives out money for certain special purposes 基金会
  e.g. The Gulbenkian Foundation gives money to help artists.

 

get in
become accepted for membership or chosen for office 被接受或录用
  e.g. He took the entrance exam but didn't get in.

 

  

take away
take from a person or place 带走
  e.g. Two chicken curries and rice to take away.

 

scrap
n. a small piece or bit 小块，少许
  e.g. Give the scraps to the dog.

 

  

wrench
vt. twist or turn suddenly and forcibly 猛扭，猛拉
  e.g. He managed to wrench himself free.

 

brand
n. 商标，牌子
  e.g. What brand of soap do you like?

 

   occupy
vt. fill (time, space, etc.) 占据（时间、地点等）
  e.g. Her time is fully occupied with her three children.

 

outer
a. on the outside 在外的，外面的
  e.g. The outer walls of the house were made of brick.

 

  

administration
n. 行政（部门）；管理
  e.g. Opposite the clinic is the administration building.

 

carve
vt. make (a special shape) by cutting 雕刻
  e.g. The talented artist carved an interesting decoration from a piece of tree root.

 

  

coil
vi. wind into a series of circles 盘绕
  e.g. The snake coiled round the tree.  

 

intricate
a. very complicated 复杂的，错综的
  e.g. He is reading a novel with an intricate plot.  

 

 

impression
n. a feeling about sb. or sth. 印象
  e.g. What were your first impressions of London?

 

come at
advance towards; attack 向…走来；袭击
  e.g. She came at me with a rolling-pin.

 

   quiver
vi. shake 颤抖，抖动
  e.g. The leaves quivered in the breeze.

 

dictate
v. say (words) aloud for sb. else to write down 口述而由别人记录
  e.g. The French teacher dictates to the class every other week.

 

  

benign
a. kind, gentle 仁慈的，和蔼的
  e.g. They are a more benign audience.

 

take on
employ 雇用
  e.g. She was taken on as a graduate trainee.

 

  

put in a word (for sb.)
praise in order to recommend, etc. 为（某人）说情，帮（某人）说话
  e.g. I hope you get the job. I'll put in a word for you.
