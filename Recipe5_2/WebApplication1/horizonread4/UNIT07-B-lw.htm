New Words

prodigy
n.[C] a person who has unusual and very noticeable abilities
奇才，天才
He is likely to be very bright and find learning so easy that he becomes a prodigy or "genius" at mathematics.
他可能是非常聪明的，会觉得学习这么容易而成为数学上的一名奇才或"天才"。
However, if a child could pick up the language merely by exposure he would be considered a real prodigy.
然而，如果一名儿童仅仅通过接触就能学会这门语言，他会被视作是一个真正的奇才。

spring up
appear or develop quickly and/or suddenly
涌现，发生，迅速长出
His success doesn't spring up out of nowhere. As he self-studied College English in high school, he has had a head start over his peers.
他的成功不是凭空而来的。他在中学就自学了大学英语，所以比其他同学领先了一步。
If the project's a success, similar constructions may spring up elsewhere in the country.
如果这个项目成功的话，类似建筑物也许会在这国家其他的地方涌现。

out of nowhere
happening or appearing suddenly and without warning
不知打哪儿来，突然冒出来
He was driving too fast on the express highway when a police car appeared out of nowhere and stopped him.
他正在高速公路上超速行驶，这时不知打哪儿冒出一辆警车把他拦住了。
The thought swam into her mind out of nowhere and she realized it was a great relief to have this as an excuse.
这想法不知从何处涌入她的脑海，她意识到有了这种理由心里觉得大大地宽慰了。

look into
examine
调查，仔细检查
Many such crimes are looked into by special agencies rather than the police.
许多这种犯罪是由专门的机构，而不是由警方进行调查的。
The union representative is looking into the case to see if the company is responsible, but at the moment it rather looks as if it was entirely Len's fault.
工会代表正在调查这起案子，察看公司是否负有责任，但是目前看上去好像完全是莱恩的过错。

go out of one's way
make a special effort, esp. in spite of difficulties
特别费心（作某事），千方百计
Mary always went out of her way to help me learn Russian before I went to Moscow where I soon picked it up.
过去，玛丽总是不怕麻烦地帮助我学习俄语。等我到了莫斯科后，我很快就学会了这门语言。
He could have complained to the top about my basic lack of professionalism but instead he went out of his way to try and help me.
他原本可以向上层领导埋怨我缺乏基本的职业技艺，可相反，他千方百计地试图帮助我。

make a note of
record (sth. that one wishes to remember)
把（某事）记录下来
While reading, make a note of any page of special importance.
阅读时，任何有特别重要意义的那一页都要记下。
Make a note of how much money you spend on the trip.
把旅途中的开支记录下来。

pick up
get information or a skill by chance rather than by making a deliberate effort
学到，获得
I picked up quite a lot of Italian when I was in Italy.
我在意大利时学会了不少意大利语。
As a mail boy, he had picked up the habit of addressing people by their Christian names, whoever they were.
作为一个邮递员，他养成了用教名称呼任何人的习惯，不管他们是谁。

have a head start
have an advantage over others in a particular activity, esp. as a result of starting doing it before them
有先起步的优势，领先
If you are lucky enough to have your own land, you have a head start over those of us who have to rent facilities.
如果你有幸拥有自己的土地，比我们中那些不得不租用设施的人一开始就有利了。
He had a head start in that he had been a manager assistant for 5 years.
他一开始就有优势，因为他当过五年经理助理。

in question
under consideration; being talked about
谈论中的，考虑之中的
If the restaurant in question adds a service charge, then there would be fewer customers.
要是那家谈论中的餐馆增收服务费，那么顾客就会比较少了。
Can you give us more information about the man in question?
关于这个人你能给我们提供更多的情况吗？

pass on
give or leave to people who are younger or live later; give sth. to sb. else
把……传给……
Genetics deals with how genes are passed on from parents to their offspring and a great deal is known about the mechanisms governing this process.
遗传学涉及的是基因是如何从父母亲遗传给其后代的，人们对控制这一过程的机理已经了解了许多。
Colds and measles may be passed on easily at school, but NOT HIV.
感冒和麻疹在学校里很容易传来传去，但是艾滋病病毒并不容易传播。

step by step
gradually
一步步，逐渐地
Step by step, they are approaching the crux of the problem.
一步步地，他们正接近问题的关键。
The gang of robbers approached with guns, forcing them step by step into the sea.
那帮强盗拿着枪逼近，强迫他们一步一步地走到大海中。

have an advantage over
be better, more effective, and more useful than
比……处于有利地位，比……有优势
She also realized that she had an advantage over her mother's generation because she was a working woman and she could do things in her retirement.
她也意识到她比自己母亲这代人有优势，因为她是一个有工作的妇女，而且退休后还能做事。
His recent research finds that older learners have an advantage over the very young in learning rule-governed aspects of the language.
他最近的研究发现在学习语言的有规则的方面，年龄较大的学习者比年龄非常小的有优势。

in a (good) position to do
having enough authority or power to do
能够（做某事），有条件（做某事）
He will then be in a good position to take over much of the rest of the property when his parents die.
他父母过世时，他就能够接管其余的很大一部分财产。
As a minister, judge and professor, I feel I am in a good position to speak about the history of marriage, its importance in society, and the duties of the married couple to each other and the wider community.
作为一名牧师、法官和教授，我感到我能够来谈论婚姻的历史、婚姻在社会中的重要性，以及已婚夫妇对双方和社区的责任。

far from
not at all
远远不，一点也不
Far from damaging the environment, the expanded road program will improve the quality of life of thousands of people.
扩展道路计划非但不会对环境造成破坏，反而将改善成千上万人的生活质量。
His whole future, far from being secure, was now endangered because of what he had done.
由于他所做的一切，他的整个未来非但不可靠，现在反而处于危险之中。

Language Points

Language Point 1
It is a popular myth that great geniuses — the Einsteins, Picassos and Mozarts of this world — spring up out of nowhere as if touched by the finger of God. (Para.1)
Meaning: Most people mistakenly believe that great geniuses such as Einstein, Picasso and Mozart appear suddenly and unexpectedly and they seem as if they were created by God.

Language Point 2
…spring up out of nowhere… (Para.1)
spring up: appear or develop quickly and/or suddenly 
For some unknown reason prickles sprang up all over her shoulders and down the length of both arms. 不知道什么原因，她的整个肩膀和两条胳膊突然感到刺痛。 
Where a panel is composed of just two people, a direct rivalry may spring up about who is in charge. 如果一个讨论组只有两个人组成，就会出现谁来负责的直接竞争。

Language Point 3
…spring up out of nowhere… (Para.1)
out of nowhere: appear suddenly and unexpectedly 
The thought swam into her mind out of nowhere and she felt vaguely guilty when she realised it was a great relief to have this excuse. 这个想法不知怎么冒了出来，当她意识到找这个借口是一种逃脱的时候，隐约感到内疚。 
Unaccompanied children also cross this crossing and they are very vulnerable to vehicles which seem to appear out of nowhere. 没人陪伴的小孩也过这个十字路口。这时，他们很容易被不知道从哪儿冒出的车撞倒。

Language Point 4
…supposedly born into a family of manual workers, … ( Para.1)
supposedly: ad. according to what is generally assumed or believed (often used to indicate that the speaker doubts the truth of the statement) 
He was supposedly delivering some papers to her but I think it was just an excuse to see her. 据说他正在给她送一些文章，但我认为这只是他想见她的借口。 
In public they have to boast about the training revolution they are supposedly leading. 在公开场合他们不得不吹嘘据称是由他们领导的培训革命。 
There is a supposedly simple explanation for what happened. 据说对发生的事有一个简单的解释。

Language Point 5
A professor who studies early learning has attacked this myth, … (Para. 2)
Meaning: A professor who specializes in the education of children at their early age has spoken strongly against this myth.

Language Point 6
… when he looked into Gauss's childhood, … (Para.2)
look into: examine 
The Inquiry looked into the problem of understaffing in many colleges and tried to find a solution. 咨询机构调查了很多高校面临的人员配备不足的问题，并努力寻求解决的办法。 
The case is being looked into; when we have anything to report we'll write to them. 案子正在调查中，如果有什么情况我们可以书面向他们报告。

Language Point 7
His father had supervised manual workers, not been one, … (Para. 2)
Meaning: His father had watched over manual workers to make certain they worked properly but he himself was not a manual worker, …

Language Point 8
His father had supervised manual workers, not been one, … (Para. 2)
supervise: v. watch over (an activity or job) to make certain that it is done correctly, or watch over (sb.) to make certain that they are behaving correctly or are safe 
The UN is supervising the distribution of aid to those areas worst affected by the fighting. 联合国负责监督分配援助物资给受战争影响最严重的地区。
Her new job involves supervising the company's operation in Japan. 她的新工作包括监督公司在日本的经营业务。
The teachers take it in turn to supervise the children at playtime. 游戏时间内老师们轮流指导孩子们的活动。

Language Point 9
It is the same story with other geniuses. (Para.3)
Meaning: Einstein, Picasso and Mozart's growing-up stories are very similar to that of Gauss. It's environment not genes that creates a genius.

Language Point 10
…who fascinated his son… (Para.3)
fascinate: v. attract or interest sb. very much 
I was fascinated by Charlotte's stories of life in Hollywood. 夏洛特在好莱坞生活的故事深深吸引了我。
He was fascinated to discover that they had both been born in the same town on the same day. 他饶有兴趣地发现他们两人是同一天在同一个镇上出生的。 
Anything to do with aeroplanes and flying fascinates him.凡是跟飞机和飞行有关的东西都让他着迷。

Language Point 11
…with practical displays of physics. (Para.3)
display: n. an occasion or performance to show sth. happening, or a particular quality, feeling 
There will be a big firework display for the President's birthday. 总统生日那天将有大型礼花表演。
The festival of Luchia is a dazzling display of the art, music and dance of an　ancient culture. 露切亚节展示了令人眩目的古代艺术、音乐和舞蹈文化。

Language Point 12
" In every case, when you look into the backgrounds of great people, there is this pattern of very early stimulation by a parent or teacher figure," the professor says. (Para.3)
Meaning: Invariably, the professor says, when you closely examine the great people's backgrounds, there is always a parent or a person who serves as a teacher supporting and guiding them at a very early period.

Language Point 13
"…there is this pattern of very early stimulation by a parent or teacher figure," the professor says. (Para. 3)
stimulation: n. arousal, encouragement 
Even as a child, he received powerful stimulation from his teachers. 甚至当他是个孩子时，他就从老师们那儿得到了巨大的激励。 
Foreign travel can act as a great stimulation for the mind. 国外旅游能大大激发人们的新思路。
Economists have advised the government to reduce interest rates in order to give some kind of stimulation to the economy. 经济学家建议政府降低利率来刺激经济发展。

Language Point 14
There is plenty of evidence that, too often, pressure from parents results in children suffering fatigue rather than becoming geniuses. (Para.4)
Meaning: Much evidence points to the fact that, quite often, parental pressure leads not so much to a child's intelligence as to his tiredness.

Language Point 15
…suffering fatigue… (Para.4)
fatigue: n. a feeling of great tiredness 
Symptoms of the illness include fever, fatigue, and loss of appetite. 这种病的症状是发烧、疲劳和没有胃口。
Long hours spent in front of a computer screen can cause fatigue of the eye muscles. 长时间坐在电脑前会令眼部肌肉疲劳。 
Increasing numbers of people in high positions are suffering from fatigue and stress-related illnesses. 越来越多担任高级职务的人感到劳累不堪，而且患有与压力有关的疾病。

Language Point 16
One study has identified two kinds of parent style… (Para. 4)
identify: v. discover or notice the existence of sth.; recognize sth. or sb. 
After years of research scientists have identified the virus that is responsible for the disease.  经过多年研究，科学家们找到了引发该疾病的病毒。 
Experts are examining the engine but the cause of the problem has not yet been identified.  专家们正在检查引擎，但是毛病还未找到。 
The airline hopes that most of those who died in the crash will be identified by their families. 航空公司希望家属能辨认出大部分死于空难的人。

Language Point 17
Supportive parents were those who would go out of their way to help their children follow their favorite interests and praised whatever level of achievement resulted. (Para.5)
Meaning: Supportive parents were those who would make a special effort to help their children do whatever they find great interest in and praise even very small progress ever made. 

Language Point 18
…who would go out of their way… (Para.5)
go out of one's way: make a special effort to help sb. even when this causes one difficulty or inconvenience
My friend goes out of his way to pick me up to play golf. 我的朋友特地来接我去打高尔夫球。 
Jennifer went out of her way to help Vicky to feel comfortable in her new job. 詹尼弗想尽办法帮助维基适应新工作。 
They really went out of their way to make us feel welcome. 他们确实是尽了全力让我们感受到欢迎。

Language Point 19
…steering them towards certain fields… (Para.5)
steer: v. take control of a situation and influence the way in which it develops 
He's very good at steering the conversation so that we talk about him. 他很擅长引导谈话内容，因此我们谈起了他。
She has steered the company away from financial ruin. 她领导公司避免了财政危机。
He successfully steered the company onto a new course.他成功地将公司带上了新路。

Language Point 20
The children were given electronic devices; … (Para. 6)
Compare "device" with "devise" 
device: n. new invention, means of doing sth. 
devise: vt. invent 
He invented a device for warning pilots when there was bad weather ahead. 他发明的一种装置可以在前方天气恶劣时向飞行员发出预警。 
A device can be attached to a private telephone which keeps a record of all calls made and their cost. 有一种装置可以安装在私人电话上，记录所有打出去的电话和电话费用。 
Anyone who can devise a means of recording television programs without recording the advertisements will make a fortune. 谁能创造一种办法在收录电视节目时不收录广告，谁就会发财。 
He managed to devise a system of bonus payments to encourage hardworking staff. 他设计了一种奖金制度以激励工作努力的员工。

Language Point 21
…when these made a sound, they had to make a note of what they were doing and assess how happy and alert they felt. (Para.6)
Meaning: …when these made a sound, they had to write down /record what they were doing and make a judgment about how happy and watchful they felt.

Language Point 22
…when these made a sound, they had to make a note of what they were doing and assess how happy and alert they felt. (Para.6)
make a note of: record (sth. that one wishes to remember) 
He made a mental note of the title.他记住了标题。 
Will all members please make a note of the date of our next meeting? 请全体成员记下我们下次会议的日期。
I made a careful note of where I had left the bag, so as to be sure to find it on my return. 我仔细地记下了我把包放在哪儿，这样回来时肯定能找到它。 
Compare:
take a note of, take note of 
I'll just take a note of your name and address. 我要记下你的姓名和地址。
You should take note of what she tells you because she knows their strategy well. 你应该留意她对你说的话，因为她很清楚他们的策略。

Language Point 23
…and assess how happy and alert they felt. (Para.6)
assess: v. 
1) make a judgement about the nature or quality of sb. or sth. 
The booklet aims to help parents assess recent educational changes. 这本小册子的目标是帮助家长评估近来的教育变化。
This test provides an excellent way of assessing applicants' suitability. 这场考试是评估应聘者是否适应这份工作的很好的方法。
2) calculate the amount or value of sth. 
I took the ring to the jeweller to have its value assessed. 我把戒指拿到珠宝商那儿去估价。
The total value of the painting is assessed at $20 million. 这幅画的总价估计是2,000万美元。

Language Point 24
…and assess how happy and alert they felt. (Para.6)
alert: a. able to think quickly; quick to notice things 
An alert postman noticed two men breaking into the house. 一个警觉性很高的邮递员注意到了有两名男子正在闯入那幢房子。
After the recent bomb attacks, the public are being warned to be alert at all times and to look out for any suspicious packages. 炸弹袭击事件后，公众被提醒要时刻保持警惕，注意任何可疑邮件包裹。 
v. warn sb. about a dangerous or urgent situation 
When he realized that one engine was not working the pilot alerted air traffic control center. 当飞行员意识到一个引擎坏了时，他通知了空中交通管制中心。 
n. a situation in which people are watching for danger and ready to deal with it
Security forces were on full alert against a possible terrorist attack. 安全部队对可能发生的恐怖袭击保持了高度警戒。

Language Point 26

Meaning: Children with demanding but impatient parents were most likely to get exhausted.

Language Point 28

have a head start: have an advantage over others in a particular activity, esp. 
as a result of starting doing it before others

Electronics is a very competitive business and the Japanese have a head start over us. 电子行业竞争非常激烈，日本已走在了我们前面。

Language Point 30
…who demonstrated how to think… (Para.9)

President Bush was anxious to demonstrate that he had a strong foreign policy. 布什总统急于表明他的外交政策是强硬的。
2) take part in a public meeting or march, usu. as a protest or to show support for sth. 
Thousands of workers and students demonstrated against U.S. involvement in the war. 成千上万的工人和学生游行示威，反对美国卷入战争。

in question: under consideration; being talked about

Language Point 33

Language Point 42
The most significant implication would seem to be that while most people are in a good position to fulfill their biological potential —barring serious illnesses or a poor diet during childhood — it is far from certain that they will grow up in an environment where that capacity will be developed. (Para.14)
Meaning: Biologically speaking, most people, except for those who are seriously ill or undernourished at a young age, would seem to be able to take good advantage of their genes. Most significantly, however, they can not be guaranteed to realize their genetic potential unless they're born into a family with both supportive and stimulating parents.
