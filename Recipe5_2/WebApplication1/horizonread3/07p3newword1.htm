campus
n. 校园
  e.g. Private automobiles are not allowed on campus.

  

take on
begin to have (a particular quality or appearance) 呈现，具有
  e.g. When some metals are combined in this way, the alloy takes on new physical properties.

 

institution
n. 机构；学校
  e.g. The Hong Kong Bank is Hong Kong's largest financial institution.

 

  

myth
n. 杜撰出来的人（或事）；神话
  e.g. Most societies have their own creation myths.

 

mobile
a. that can move or be moved easily from place to place 流动的；可移动的
  e.g. The mobile medical team will soon be here.

 

  

cash in on
take advantage of 利用，从……中得到好处
  e.g. The company cashed in on its rival's difficulties by doubling its production.

 

handy
a. skilful in using one's hands to make things 手巧的
  e.g. Having lived in China for almost two years, Paul is now very handy with his chopsticks.

 

   advertise
v. praise publicly in order to persuade people to buy or use (sth.) 做广告，宣传
  e.g. We should advertise for someone to look after our children.

 

come down to
be able to be summarized as 可归结为
  e.g. What it comes down to is a choice between cutting wages or reducing the number of staff.

 

  

trivial
a. of little importance 琐碎的，不重要的
  e.g. I'm sorry to bother you with such a trivial problem.

 

push around
order (sb.) around 将……差来差去；欺负
  e.g. I wish you would stop pushing little Jack around all the time.

 

  

settle into
become used to (living in a new home, doing a new job, etc.) 开始适应，习惯于
  e.g. We settled the children into new schools when we moved to London.  

 

management
n. the control and making of decisions in a business 管理
  e.g. The scientific management method has brought about many changes in factory production.  

 

 

once in a while
occasionally 偶尔
  e.g. I still see my ex-wife once in a while.

 

put through
cause (sb.) to undergo (a test, etc.); arrange for (sb.) to complete his education 使经受（考验）；使完成学业
  e.g. He put all his children through boarding-school（寄宿制学校）.

 

   criticize
vt. express a judgment on a work of art, etc. 批评，评论
  e.g. His policies were severely criticized.

 

blunt
a. frank and direct 直言不讳的
  e.g. She is blunt about her personal life.

 

  

issue
n. an important topic for discussion or argument 问题，争论点
  e.g. What issue are Linda and her husband arguing about now?

 

fantastic
a. extremely good; excellent 极好的
  e.g. She's a fantastic swimmer.

 

  

for once
on this occasion only, as an exception 就此一次，破例一次
  e.g. Just for once he arrived on time.
