aim at
瞄准；对准
  e.g. He aimed at the target.

  

take the plunge
take a bold decisive step, esp. after thinking about it for some time（尤指经过一番思考后）采取断然行动
  e.g. He took the plunge by investing all his savings in the stock market.

 

aggressive
a. (derog) always ready to quarrel or attack; threatening 爱挑衅的，好斗的；侵略的
  e.g. They were drunk, aggressive and looking for a fight.

 

  

in one's favor
to the advantage of sb. 对某人有利
  e.g. The protection that farmers have enjoyed amounts to a bias in favor of the countryside.

 

head for
move towards (a place) 向（某处）行进
  e.g. These people are heading for the city hall.

 

  

depart
vi. 死，去世；离开，起程
  e.g. When does the next train depart?

 

confirm
vt. provide evidence for the truth of; establish the truth of 证实；确认
  e.g. Please confirm your telephone message by writing to me.

 

   inflict
vt. force (sth. unwanted or unpleasant) on 使遭受
  e.g. The sufferings inflicted on the children were terrible to see.

 

circumstance
n. (usu. pl.) condition or fact connected with another event or action 环境；情形；情况
in/under the/no circumstances 在这种情况下／在任何情况下决不
  e.g. Maybe under these circumstances we may say that man can conquer nature.

 

  

advocate
vt. speak in favor of; support 主张；提倡
  e.g. Our premier advocates higher salaries for teachers.

 

conclude
vt. come to believe (sth.) as a result of reasoning; reach a decision; bring to an end 断定，得出结论；决定；结束
  e.g. The doctor concluded that the patient's disease was cancer.

 

  

insure
vt. (AmE) make sure; guarantee 确保；保证
  e.g. Carefulness insures you against errors.

 

count on
rely on 依靠；指望
  e.g. We can count on Lisa to keep her promise.

 

 

recommend
vt. make a favorable judgment of 推荐
  e.g. Can you recommend a good dictionary?

 

impact
n. the force of one object hitting another 冲击力
  e.g. Under the impact of the waves, rocks along the coast have lost their edges.

 

  

target
n. anything fired at in shooting practice 靶；目标
  e.g. The hunter's target was a wild animal.

draw the line at sth./doing sth.
refuse to do or refuse to tolerate sth. 拒绝做某事；决不容忍某事
  e.g. The criminal declared that he would draw the line at hitting an old lady.

 

  

break in/into
enter by force 强行进入
  e.g. The burglar broke in and stole my money.

 

on the line
(infml) at risk 冒险
  e.g. He wouldn't put his career on the line to help a friend.
