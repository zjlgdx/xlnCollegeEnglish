have second thought(s) on
change opinion after considering (sth.) again （重新考虑后）改变主意
  e.g. I have never had second thoughts about my decision to leave the company.

  

prospect
n. the chance or hope that sth. will happen 前景，可能性
  e.g. We have good prospects.

 

enhance
vt. improve further the good quality or status of 增强
  e.g. Passing the English examination should enhance your chances of getting the post.

 

  

artificial
a. made or produced as a copy of sth. natural; not real 人工的；假的
  e.g. The city is dotted with many small artificial lakes.

 

subscribe to
have (a particular opinion); arrange to receive a newspaper, etc. regularly by paying in advance 赞成；订阅
  e.g. Personally I subscribe to your opinion.

 

  

necessarily
ad. as an inevitable result; in all cases 必然地
  e.g. The fact that something is cheap doesn't necessarily mean it's of low quality.

 

condemn
vt. disapprove of strongly 谴责
  e.g. Most people are willing to condemn violence of any sort as evil.

 

   vague
a. not clearly expressed or stated 模糊的
  e.g. Through the fog we saw the vague outline of a ship.

 

lend support to
help 支持
  e.g. He was approached by the organizers to lend support to a benefit concert.

 

  

objection
n. expression of opposition, disapproval or dislike 反对
  e.g. He has a strong objection to building a new trade center in such a remote area.

 

come to terms with
accept (sth) unpleasant over a period of time; come to an agreement with 只好接受，顺从；与……达成协议
  e.g. It's hard to come to terms with being unemployed.

 

  

inevitable
a. impossible to avoid; certain to happen 不可避免的
  e.g. Such a difficult operation may not succeed; it's an inevitable gamble.  

 

be bound to
be certain to 一定会
  e.g. I'll show it to Benjamin. He's bound to know.  

 

 

in essence
essentially; fundamentally 实质上
  e.g. The two arguments are in essence the same.

 

overcome
(overcame; overcome) vt. get the better of; conquer 克服，征服
  e.g. We overcame a lot of difficulties.

 

   by virtue of
on account of; because of 由于
  e.g. She became a British citizen by virtue of marriage.

 

prohibit
vt. forbid, esp. by laws 禁止
  e.g. Smoking is prohibited in most theaters.

 

  

rest on
be based on 依赖
  e.g. His whole argument rested on false assumption.

 

vital
a. of crucial importance; essential 极其重要的
  e.g. Such measures are vital to national security.

 

  

significant
a. important; considerable 重要的，重大的
  e.g. It is significant for a country simply to participate in the Olympics.
