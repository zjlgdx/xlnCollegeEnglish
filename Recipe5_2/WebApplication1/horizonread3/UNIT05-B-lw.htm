New Words

stroke
n.1. [C] a sudden change in the blood supply to a part of the brain, which can cause loss of the ability to move, speak clearly, etc.
中风
She had a stroke which left her unable to speak.
她中风后不能开口说话了。
She had a stroke and was unable to walk for the rest of her life.
她突然中风，此后的岁月中再也无法行走了。
2. [C] a blow, esp. with (the edge of) a weapon
击，打，敲
He split the log with one stroke of the axe.
他一斧劈开了那木料。
The thief was caught on the spot and received several strokes of the whip.
小偷被当场抓住，挨了几鞭。
vt.pass the hand gently over (a surface), usu. again and again
抚摸
She lovingly stroked Chris's face with the tips of her fingers.
她充满爱意地用手指尖抚摸克里斯的脸庞。
The girl stroked the cat.
那女孩轻抚着猫。

worthless
a.having no value or usefulness
无价值的，没有用处的
You must be careful not to be left with worthless shares.
你必须小心不要留有那些无价值的股票。
A $30 million fund has been created for those whose tickets became worthless following the collapse of the airline company.
一项3,000万美元的基金已建立起来，以补偿因航空公司倒闭而导致机票失去价值的人们。

dependent
a.1. needing support from sb.
依赖的，依靠的
He has three dependent children.
他有三个要抚养的孩子。
West Europe was still heavily dependent on Middle East oil.
西欧仍然在很大程度上依赖中东的石油。
2. affected or decided by sth.
取决于... ...的
It is largely dependent on the weather.
这多半取决于天气情况。
Promotion in that company is dependent on hard work.
要在那家公司获得晋升就得努力工作。

x-ray
n.1. [C] X光照片
The doctor examined the x-rays, to see if they showed anything wrong with her lungs.
医生检查了这些X光片，看它们是否显示出她的肺有什么毛病。
The x-ray showed that the bone was not broken.
X光片显示骨头没有断。
We'll take an x-ray of your chest.
我们将对你的胸部进行X光检查。
2. [C] X射线; X光
X-rays can pass through some solid materials.
X射线能够透过某些固体物质。
X-rays are used to locate breaks in bones, bullets lodged in the body, and to treat certain diseases.
X光被用来确定断骨和体内子弹的位置，并用于治疗某些疾病。

infection
n.1. [C] a disease caused by the movement of an organism into a living body
传染病
People catch all kinds of infections in the winter.
人们在冬天易患各种各样的传染病。
He had an ear infection that made him stone deaf.
他得过传染性耳疾，以致耳朵全聋了。
2. [U] becoming ill through contact with such a disease
传染，感染
She was afraid of carrying the infection to her child.
她怕把疾病传染给孩子。
We should try to prevent young people from the infection of dangerous ideas.
我们应该尽力避免年轻人被危险的思想腐蚀。

germ
n.1. [C] a very small organism that causes disease
微生物，病菌，细菌
Wash your hands so you don't get germs on the food.
洗洗手，这样你就不会把细菌带到食物上。
The germs are easily passed from person to person.
病菌很容易从一个人传给另一个人。
This disinfectant kills most germs.
这种消毒剂可以杀死大多数病菌。
Is this milk free from germs?
这种牛奶没有细菌吗？
Cover your mouth when you cough so that you won't spread germs.
咳嗽的时候捂住嘴，这样你就不会传播病菌了。
2. [C] the beginning from which sth. may develop
萌芽，起源
Looking for a way to make money, he found the germ of an idea in an old newspaper.
他在寻求生财之道，结果从一张旧报纸上得到启发，萌生了一个想法。
It contains the germ of an idea.
它包含着某种思想的萌芽。

deny
vt.1. refuse to give sb., or prevent sb. from having (sth. asked for or wanted)
拒绝给予，拒绝……的要求

她很生气，因为没给她机会见见我。

2. not admit

3. state a punishment for (a guilty person); sentence

这名囚犯被判死刑。
The judge condemned her to spend 6 years in prison.
法官判她六年徒刑。

theoretical
a.1. concerned with the theory of a subject; supposed but not necessarily true
理论（上）的，假设的，推理的

这本书过于注重理论方面，我需要一本实用指南。

他的天赋使他在推理上占了优势。
2. based on ideas and principles, not on practical experience
根据理论（而非实践）的

理论物理
a theoretical basis
理论基础
A lot of university courses are still too theoretical these days.
现在很多大学课程仍然偏重学习理论。

hell
n.1. [U] (in some religions) the place where some people are believed to go after death to be punished forever for the bad things they have done during their lives
地狱
a living hell
人间地狱
They believed that some people would go to hell when they died.
他们相信，一些人死后会下地狱。
2. [U] an extremely unpleasant or difficult place, situation or experience
极不愉快的经历（或事）

此刻的工作如同在地狱一般，因为两台电脑都坏了。
It was absolute hell trying to get this report finished on time.
要准时完成这份报告可真是苦差使。
3. (used as a swear-word to show anger or surprise or for emphasis)
用以表示愤怒或惊讶，或用以加强语气
Oh hell, I've forgotten my money!
该死，我没带钱！
Go to hell! (=Go away!)
滚开！
Who the hell is at the front door?
到底谁在前门？

不成功的，无效的，没有意义的

她属于那种爱虚荣的人，每到一面镜子前都非要照一照不可。
She is vain of her beauty.
她为自己的美貌而自负。

困难总是在你最意想不到的时候突然出现。
Take every chance that comes along.
抓住一切偶然出现的机会。

on the one hand …on the other hand…
(used to indicate contrasting points of view, opinions, etc.)
一方面... ...另一方面... ...
On the one hand I want to buy this new car, on the other hand I feel that it costs too much money.
一方面我想买下这辆新车，但另一方面我又觉得太贵了。
On the one hand we could stay and help you, but on the other hand, it might be better if we went to help him instead.
一方面我们可以留下来帮助你，但另一方面，如果我们去帮助他，也许会更好些。

condemn sb. to sth.
make sb. take or accept sth. unpleasant
使某人做不愿做的事，把某人逼入某种状态
His bad leg condemns him to a wheelchair.
他那条有残疾的腿迫使他坐轮椅。
When the poor woman lost her husband, she realized that she was condemned to a lonely old age.
这个可怜的女人丧偶后，意识到自己注定要度过孤独的晚年。

take its course
develop naturally and come to a natural end
任其自然发展，按常规进行
We can't cure the disease; it must take its course.
我们无法治愈这种病，只好听其自然了。
Once a fight starts, it must take its course unless it is stopped by outside interference.
打斗一旦开始就不好收场，除非有外部干预。

fight off
keep sth. away with an effort; struggle to prevent sth. unwanted or avoid someone unwanted
抵抗，击退，避开
He fought off three men who attacked him.
他击退了三个攻击他的人。
Did your husband manage to fight off his cold by taking medicine?
你丈夫服药后感冒好了吗？

be responsible for
be the cause of esp. sth. bad
对……负有责任
A drunken man cannot be fully responsible for his actions.
醉汉不能对其行为负全部责任。
Who is responsible for the broken windows?
谁把玻璃打破了？

in vain
without a successful result; uselessly
无结果地，无用地
We tried to persuade him not to go swimming, but in vain.
我们力图说服他不去游泳，可他就是不听。
It was in vain that we tried to find his mother.
我们设法寻找他的母亲，但毫无结果。

come by
obtain, usu. by effort
努力获得
How did you come by that beautiful picture?
你是怎样弄到那幅美丽的图画的？
A good job that you enjoy doing is hard to come by.
你喜欢干的工作是不容易找到的。

chances are (that…)
it is likely that…
可能
Chances are that she'll be coming.
她可能会来。
Chances are he's already heard the news.
可能他已经听到了这个消息。

confront with
face with
使面对（问题、挑战等）
The writer was confronted with a pile of work.
这位作家面对一大堆工作。
The hunters were fearless when confronted with wild animals.
这些猎手面对野兽毫不畏惧。

die of
die because of (sth. such as an illness or feeling)
死于
She just died of old age.
她死于衰老。
In a severe winter, many wild animals can die of hunger.
严冬时，许多野兽可能饿死。

come down to
be able to be concluded as (sth.); be a question of (sth.)
归结为，实质上是
The whole matter comes down to a power struggle between the trade union and the directors.
整个事情归结起来就是工会与厂长们之间的权力之争。
What it all comes down to is that there isn't much in his story.
归结下来，他的叙述没有什么内容。

bar… from
forbid someone from sth. or doing sth.
禁止某人做某事
After the member was caught cheating, he was barred from the club.
在被发现舞弊之后，这个成员被驱出了俱乐部。
The doctor was barred from practising after he was proved guilty of improper behaviour.
在被证明有不当行为后，这个医生被禁止行医。

Language Points

Language Point 1
Assume for a moment that your 90-year-old mother has recently suffered a stroke. (Para. 1)
Meaning: Suppose for a moment that your 90-year-old mother has recently had a stroke.

Language Point 2
Assume for a moment that your 90-year-old mother has recently suffered a stroke. (Para. 1)
assume: vt. take as a fact or as true without proof; suppose
When you have a language degree, people assume that you can speak the language fluently. 如果你获得某种语言的学位，人们就会认为你能流利地说这种语言。
I assume you don't drive, Mr. Sharp? 我想您不会开车，是吧，夏普先生？

Language Point 3
Assume for a moment that your 90-year-old mother has recently suffered a stroke. (Para. 1)
stroke: n.
1) a sudden change in the blood supply to a part of the brain, which can cause loss of the ability to move, speak clearly, etc.
Mary likes to visit her old friends and also travels to see her brother who has had a stroke recently. 玛丽想去拜访她的老朋友，也想去看望她的哥哥，他最近中风了。
2) a blow, esp. with (the edge of) a weapon
He drove in the nail with only one stroke of the hammer. 他一锤就把钉子钉了进去。
vt. pass the hand gently over (a surface), usu. again and again
She held me tenderly in her arms and stroked my hair, and said nothing until I was calm again. 她温柔地将我搂在怀里，抚摸着我的头发，一言不发，直到我安静下来为止。
Jack stroked the watch affectionately with his finger and returned it to his pocket.杰克用手指爱惜地抚摸这块手表，然后将它放回口袋里。

Language Point 4

worthless: a. having no value or usefulness

Your conclusions are far from worthless; they make a good deal of sense to me. 你的结论远不是毫无价值，对于我来说，它们意义重大。

Language Point 9

You see, we have problems of our own. 你知道，我们也有自己的问题。

Language Point 13

Language Point 24

Language Point 25
…limited life to which the stroke has condemned her. (Para. 5)
condemn: vt. criticize strongly, usu. for moral reasons
The author condemned racism in the strongest language in this article.在这篇文章中作者用最严厉的语言谴责了种族主义。
East and West leaders join in condemning violence and killing. 东西方领导人联合谴责暴力和杀戮。
condemn sb. to:
1) make sb. take or accept sth. unpleasant
His broken leg condemned him to a wheelchair. 他的断腿迫使他只能坐轮椅。
I don't want to be responsible for condemning my mother to a living hell. 我不愿意承担这样的责任，使母亲处于极度痛苦之中。
2) punish sb. with sth.
In former times a murderer who was found guilty would be condemned to death. 先前，杀人犯罪名成立就会被处以死刑。
Compare:
condemn sb./sth. for: have a poor opinion of sb. or sth. because of sth. bad
The whole town condemned the girl for her wild behaviour. 这女孩因行为不检点而遭到全镇人的指责。
The city was condemned for its high crime rate. 这座城市因犯罪率高而遭到指责。

Language Point 26

Meaning: On the other hand, you hate to be the person who has to make the decision to let your mother die on her own.
let nature take its course: allow events to happen and develop naturally.
Here, of course, "nature" refers to "the life of your mother". Therefore, the implied meaning of the phrase is "let your mother's life come to a natural end" or "let your mother die on her own".

Language Point 27
On the other hand, you hate to be the one to decide to let nature take its course. (Para. 5)
take (run) its course: develop naturally and come to a natural end

Language Point 29
I'll tell you which choice I would make in this theoretical situation. (Para. 6)
theoretical: a.
1) concerned with the theory of a subject; supposed but not necessarily true

2) based on ideas and principles, not on practical experience
Eric has only a theoretical knowledge of cars. 埃里克仅有一些关于汽车的理论知识。
The belief that the universe is expanding has been proven and is no longer theoretical. 宇宙在不断扩张的观点已经得到证明，不再仅仅是理论了。
