New Words

endurance
n.[U] the state or power of enduring (sth. painful)
忍耐，忍耐力
Running is a test of human endurance and the power of mind over body.
跑步是对人类耐力和毅力战胜体力的考验。
He showed remarkable endurance throughout his illness.
在整个生病期间，他表现出非同一般的忍受能力。

moderate
a.1. average in amount, quality, etc.; not extreme
中等的，适中的，适度的
The room is of moderate size - just right for a small family.
这间屋中等大小，正好适合一个小家庭住。
It's a moderate price for a car of this type.
对于这种类型的车，这只是中等价位。
2. of or having (usu. political) opinions that are not extreme
中庸的，温和的
The deputy of the party leader is more moderate in her views.
该党派领袖的副手观点比较温和。
He is a person who is politically moderate.
他在政治上很稳健。

evidence
n.[U] one or more reasons for believing that sth. is or is not true
根据，证据，证明
All the evidence points to a substantial rise in traffic over the next few years.
所有的证据都表明，在以后的几年里交通量将大幅度增加。
How can they expect me to give evidence against my own mother?
他们怎么能指望我出示不利于我亲生母亲的证据呢？

journal
n.1. [C] a serious magazine or newspaper which is published regularly
日报，杂志，期刊
Each club member will receive a journal four times a year which contains news, reviews and articles.
每个俱乐部成员每年将收到4期刊物，内容包括新闻、评论和文章。
An article about the dangers of the new drug has recently been published in the British Medical Journal.
最近，《英国医学报》上发表了一篇关于这种新药的危害性的文章。
2. [C] a written record of what one has done each day
日记
Her journal is an important record of the life of a young woman growing up in rural England.
她的日记是一个在英格兰农村长大的年轻女孩子的生活的重要记录。
He kept a journal for over 50 years, in which he recorded his daily activities.
他写了50多年的日记，记录了每天的活动。

consume
vt.1. eat or drink
吃，喝
He consumes vast quantities of meat with every meal.
他每餐都吃大量的肉。
They spend their evenings consuming whiskey.
他们喝着威士忌消磨晚上的时间。
2. use, esp. in large amounts
消耗，花费（尤指大量地）
That's the trouble with those big powerful cars — they consume too much fuel.
这就是大马力汽车的缺点--太耗油。
Whole days were consumed by exhausting discussions.
整天整天的时间被耗费在使人身心交瘁的讨论之中。

supplement
n.1. [C] sth. added to sth. else to improve or complete it
增补(物)，补充(物)
The money I get from teaching evening classes provides a supplement to my income from my job.
我在夜校教书的收入是工资的补充。
The night courses are a supplement to his regular course work.
晚上的课程是他正常课程的补充。
2. [C] an additional part of a magazine or newspaper, either produced separately or as part of the magazine or newspaper
（杂志或报纸的）副刊，增刊
The newspaper publishes a sports supplement every Monday.
这份报纸每周一都出一份体育增刊。
Newspapers and magazines often include free supplement.
报纸和杂志经常有免费增刊。
vt.add to or complete sth. with sth. else
增补，补充
They had to get a job to supplement the family income.
他们不得不找一份工作以贴补家庭收入。
In the busy holiday season, extra buses are provided to supplement the existing service.
在繁忙的假日季节，会增开汽车以补充现有服务。

bounce
v.(cause sth. to) spring back when sent against sth. hard
（使）弹回，（使）反弹
She bounced the ball once or twice before serving.
她发球前把球弹了一两下。
The ball hit the ground and bounced high into the air.
球碰到地面又高高地弹入空中。
n.[C, U] an act of bouncing; the ability to bounce
弹，弹力
In tennis you must hit the ball before its second bounce.
网球运动中，在球第二次弹起之前必须击中它。
This ball has plenty of bounce while that one has lost its bounce.
这只球弹性很强，而那一只已全无弹性了。

respectively
ad.separately or in turn
分别地，各自地
In the 200 meters, Lisa and Rose came first and third respectively.
在200米赛跑中，丽莎和罗斯分别取得了第一名和第三名。
German and Italian courses are held in Munich and Rome respectively.
德语和意大利语课将分别在慕尼黑和罗马开设。

ratio
n.[C] the relationship between two groups or amounts, which expresses how much bigger one is than the other
比率
The school is trying to improve its pupil-teacher ratio.
学校正试图改善其学生与教师的比率。
The ratio of men to women at the meeting was ten to one.
出席会议的男女之比是10比1。

plus
conj.and
并且，而且

而且，我们也没有足够的钱去海边旅行。
prep.with the addition of

汇票是10美元，再加上1美元邮费。
There will be two adults traveling, plus three children.
将有两个大人旅行，外加三个小孩。
n.1. [C] a sign showing that two or more numbers are to be added together, or that a number is greater than zero
加号，正号
2. [C] an advantage
有利因素
Her knowledge of French is a plus in her job.
她会法语，这对她的工作很有好处。
There are various pluses for us in going to this school — it's near our house, for one.
上这所学校对我们来说有很多好处--离家近就是一点。
a.above zero; positive
正的
The temperature is plus four degrees.
温度是零上四度。

restrict
vt.put a limit on
限制，约束
The government has restricted freedom of movement into and out of the country.
政府已限制了出入境的行动自由。
We had to restrict the number of students on this course.
我们必须限制这门课程的选修人数。

liable
a. 1. likely to do sth.
易于... ...的，有... ... 倾向的
We're liable to make mistakes when we're tired.
疲劳时我们都容易出错。
Watch the milk — it's liable to boil over.
注意牛奶，它开了容易溢出来。
2. responsible by law
有法律责任的，有义务的
He is liable for all the damage done by his workmen.
他应对他的工人所造成的全部损害负责。
The law holds parents liable if a child does not attend school.
法律规定如果孩子没有上学父母将负有责任。

allowance
n.1. [C] an amount of sth. provided regularly or allowed in particular circumstances
限额，定量
You'll have to pay extra money if you exceed (超) your luggage allowance.
如果你行李超重的话，就必须额外交费。
an allowance of time for recreation
规定的娱乐时间
2. [C] amount of sth., esp. money, allowed or given regularly
津贴，补助，零用钱
While he was at college, his parents gave him such a small allowance that he had to earn extra money by working in a bar (酒吧).
他上大学的时候，父母给他的零用钱很少，他不得不在一家酒吧里打工来挣取额外的钱。

多数人出差时都能拿到出差津贴。

digest
vt.1. change (food) in the stomach so that it can be used by the body
消化
Fish is easy to digest when you're ill.
生病时，吃鱼是容易消化的。
Some foods are quicker to digest than others.
有些食物消化得比其他食物要快。
2. understand and remember
领会
Having digested the lessons of the past, we must now find new ways with which to face the future.
吸取了过去的教训后，我们现在必须找到如何面对将来的新路子。
The report contains too much to digest at one reading.
报告内容太多，读一次无法全部领会。

digestive
a.of digesting (of food)
消化的
digestive system
消化系统
digestive organs
消化器官
the digestive process
消化过程

有证据表明，读书不是近期才有的现象。

住房问题往往是一种城市现象。

在他航运事业的高峰期，他拥有60艘船。

Language Points

Language Point 1
…that endurance athletes, particularly females, frequently have iron deficiencies. (Para. 1)
Meaning: …that athletes such as long distance runners, especially women athletes, often display a lack of iron.

Language Point 2
…that endurance athletes… (Para. 1)
endurance: n. the ability to continue with an unpleasant or difficult situation, experience, or activity over a long period of time 
endurance test 耐力测试 
beyond/past endurance 超出可以忍受的限度 
The exercise obviously will improve strength and endurance. 显然，这种锻炼会增强体力和耐力。

Language Point 3
Now a new study by a team of Purdue University researchers… (Para. 1)
Meaning: Now a new study conducted by a team of researchers from Purdue University…

Language Point 4

Meaning: …suggests that even exercise of a mild type may cause a loss of iron in the blood of women.

moderate: a. of middle degree, power, or rate; neither large nor small, neither high nor low

Language Point 81
…can interfere with iron being absorbed into the body. (Para. 11)
interfere with:
1) get in the way of; prevent from working or happening 
Nothing must be allowed to interfere with our plans for the weekend. 决不允许任何事情干扰我们的周末计划。 
You mustn't allow your family duties to interfere with your work. 你不可让家庭责任干扰你的工作。 
2) touch or cause damage to (sth. which is not one’s concern) 
Somebody’s been interfering with my papers again; I do wish everyone would leave them alone, I have them carefully organized. 又有人动了我的稿子。我真的希望不要有人动它们，我都仔细地把它们按顺序放好了。 
This noise interferes with my work. 这噪声妨碍我工作。 
Compare:
interfere in: meddle in; concern oneself with and usu. take unwanted action about someone else's business 
Don’t interfere in matters that don't concern you. 不要介入那些与你无关的事。 
I never interfere in his affairs. 我从不干涉他的事。 
To interfere in something is to take part in it without being wanted. But to interfere with something is to prevent it from happening. 
interfere in sth.是指别人未请而自己主动参与；interfere with sth.是指阻碍事情发生。

Language Point 82

sum up:
1) give the main idea of sth. written or spoken; give a short account of sth. 
You have only 100 words in which to sum up his speech. 你总结他的讲话不能超过100个字。 
I can best sum up the advantages of the system by describing some recent encouraging results. 通过描述最近取得的令人鼓舞的成果，我能很好地概括该系统的长处。 
2) make a total of an amount 
These ten books sum up this year's production. 这10本书概括了今年的全部成果。 
Your marks sum up to 87. 你（一共）得了87分。
