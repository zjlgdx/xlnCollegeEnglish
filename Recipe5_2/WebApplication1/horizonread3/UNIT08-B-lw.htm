New Words

panic
n.[C, U] (a) sudden very strong feeling of anxiety and fear
恐慌，惊慌
He got in a panic that he would forget his lines on stage.
他慌张起来，怕自己到了台上会忘记台词。
They felt panic at the thought of leaving their family.
他们一想到要离开家就感到慌乱。
v.(cause a person or an animal to) be affected with panic
（使）惊慌
The crowd panicked at the sound of the explosion.
听到爆炸声，人群惊慌失措。
The thunder panicked the horse.
雷声使马受惊了。

preface
n.1. [C] an action that is intended to introduce sth. else more important
开端，前奏，序幕
We're hoping these talks could be a preface to peace.
我们希望这些会谈成为和平的序幕。
She says she has nothing to wear at the party — I think that's probably a preface to asking for a new dress.
她说她没有参加晚会可穿的衣服——我想这大概是她要求买新衣服的前奏吧。
2. [C] sth. which comes before and introduces sth. more important, esp. a piece of text at the beginning of a book
序言，前言，引言
In his preface, the author says that he took eight years to write the book.
在前言中，作者说他写这本书用了8年的时间。
He wrote a preface to the play.
他为剧本写了前言。

refusal
n.[U, C] refusing or act of refusing
拒绝
The government's refusal to see that the protection of the environment must be our first priority today is a great tragedy.
政府没能看到环境保护是我们现在最迫切的问题，这是一个大悲剧。
My offer got a cold refusal.
我的提议遭到了冷淡的拒绝。

conservative
a.tending not to like or trust change, esp. sudden change
保守的，守旧的，传统的
It's a very conservative society — they're easily shocked by anything different.
这是个极其保守的社会——任何不同的举动都让他们感到震惊。
I tend to be rather conservative in such matters.
在这种事上我趋于保守。
n.a person who is conservative
保守的人，保守派
Aunt Mary's a real conservative. She's totally opposed to women going out to work.
玛丽阿姨是一个非常保守的人，她完全反对妇女外出工作。

spiritual
a.1. of the Church or of religion
宗教上的
spiritual leader
宗教领袖
2. of the human spirit(s) or soul; not of physical things
心灵的，精神上的
Traditional ways of life provided spiritual satisfaction.
传统生活方式提供了精神上的满足。
Their friendship was strictly spiritual.
他们的友谊纯粹是精神上的。

constitute
vt.1. be or be considered as
是，构成
Nuclear weapons constitute a very real threat to world peace.
核武器构成对世界和平的真正威胁。
These questions constituted a challenge to the established order.
这些问题对已建立的秩序提出了挑战。
2. form or make up (sth.)
组成，构成
There are nearly 200 million working women in China, constituting one-sixth of the total population.
中国有两亿劳动妇女，占总人口的六分之一。
These 75,000 men constituted the whole strength of the Dutch army.
这75,000人构成了荷兰军队的全部。

gross
a.1. clearly wrong
明显错误的
The court has made a gross mistake in sending an innocent man to prison.
法庭犯了一个严重的错误，将一名无辜的人投进了监狱。
This child has suffered gross neglect.
这个孩子显然缺乏照顾。
2. total
总的，总共的
She earns $30,000 a year gross.
她一年的总收入为3万英镑。
Once wrapped, the gross weight of the package is 2kg.
包装后，这个包裹毛重2公斤。

applicable
a.that can be applied; suitable
适用的，合适的，适当的
The new policy is applicable to all European countries.
这项新政策适用于欧洲所有国家。
This part of law is only applicable to companies employing more than five people.
这一部分法律仅适用于雇佣五个人以上的公司。

voluntary
a.done, made or given willingly, without being forced or paid to do it
自愿的，志愿的
She does voluntary work for the Red Cross two days a week.
她每个星期为红十字会义务工作两天。
Is it voluntary, or do we have to go?
我们是自愿的还是必须去？

consent
n.[U] agreement
同意，准许
They can't publish your name without your consent.
未经你的同意，他们不能刊登你的名字。
My father will never give his consent to our marriage.
我父亲绝不会同意我们的婚事。
vi.give one's agreement
同意，准许
My aunt never married because her father wouldn't consent to her marriage to a cousin.
我姑妈终生未嫁，因为她父亲不同意她和她表亲结婚。
The owner wouldn't consent to our making any change in the structure of the flat.
房主不许我们对套间做任何结构上的改动。

vivid
a.producing very clear, powerful and detailed images in the mind
鲜艳的，鲜明的，生动的，逼真的
Parts of my childhood are so vivid to me that they could be memories of yesterday.
我童年时代的一些片段还是这么栩栩如生，就好像是昨天的记忆。
She was wearing a vivid pink skirt.
她穿着一条色彩鲜艳的粉红色裙子。

reflection
n.1. [U, C] thought or idea of past events
考虑，深思
After reflection, Paul decided he had been wrong to refuse the job in London.
经过深思，保罗认为他拒绝伦敦的那份工作是错误的。
After thirty years as a judge, her reflections on life and justice were well worth listening to.
她做了30年的法官，她对生活和司法的认识是很值得听一听的。
2. [C, U] reflecting or sth. reflected, esp. an image in a mirror
反射，反照；倒影
We looked at our reflections in the lake.
我们看着我们映在湖中的倒影。
She studied her reflection in the mirror.
她细看自己在镜子中的样子。
3. [C, U] sth. that shows the effects of, or is a sign of, a particular condition, situation, etc.
反映，表达
The rising rate of crime is a reflection of an unstable society.
犯罪率上升反映了社会的不稳定。
Do you think this opinion is an accurate reflection of the public mood?
你认为这意见确实反映了公众的情绪吗？

probable
a.likely to be true or likely to happen
很有可能的，大概的
The doctor said that the most probable cause of death was heart failure.
这个医生说最可能引起死亡的是心力衰竭。
An election in June seems increasingly probable.
越来越有可能在6月进行选举。

improbable
a.not probable
不大可能的，未必确实的
It's highly improbable that Nora will agree.
诺拉根本不会同意。
His excuse as to why they were late sounds improbable.
他为他们迟到所找的借口听上去不大可能。

sake
n.[C] reason
缘由，理由
You're only arguing for the sake of arguing.
你只是为争论而争论。
The company has decided for economy's sake to close down this department.
为了省钱，公司决定关闭这个部门。

surplus
a.more than is needed
过剩的，多余的
Farmers are feeding all their surplus wheat to pigs.
农民们把所有多余的小麦用来喂猪。
Every country has a lot of surplus labor.
每个国家都有大量的剩余劳动力。
n.[C, U] an amount which is more than is needed
过剩；剩余额
The world is now producing large food surpluses.
全世界的粮食产量已有大量剩余。
We cannot produce any surplus this year.
我们今年的产品不会有剩余。

soccer
n.[U] (in Britain) football
英式足球
I preferred soccer to other sports when I was in middle school.
较之其他体育项目，我在中学时更喜欢踢足球。
In soccer, only goalkeepers may use their hands to play the ball.
在足球比赛中，只有守门员才可用手接球。
I had a game of soccer with my classmates.
我和我的同学们一起踢足球。
Soccer has become the most popular outdoor activity among an increasing number of young people.
足球已经成为越来越多的年轻人最喜爱的户外运动方式。

horror
n.1. [U] (usu. pl.) anything that causes an extremely strong feeling of fear and shock
令人恐惧的事物，恐怖
The population now faces the horrors of war.
人们面临着战争的威胁。
Nuclear war is one of the horrors that face the modern world.
核战争是当今世界面临的威胁之一。
2. [U] a very strong feeling of fear and shock
恐惧，震惊
The crowd cried out in horror as the car was on fire.
汽车着火时，人们发出了惊恐的叫声。
They will never forget the blood and horror of the battle.
他们永远不会忘记这次战斗的血腥和惨状。

democratic
a.paying no or little attention to class divisions based on birth or wealth
民主的，有民主精神的
democratic centralism
民主集中制
democratic consultation
民主协商
the democratic traditions of the party
该党的民主传统
We must accept the result of a democratic election.
我们必须接受民主选举的结果。
Do you think Australia is a more democratic country than Britain?
你认为澳大利亚是个较英国更为民主的国家吗？

sexual
a.of sex or the sexes or the physical attraction between them
性的，两性的
sexual problems
性问题
sexual organs
性器官
a campaign for sexual equality
争取性平等的运动
They denied that they were having a sexual relationship.
他们否认他们之间有性关系。

sperm
n.[C] a cell produced by the sex organs of a male animal, which usu. swims in a liquid and is able to unite with the female egg to produce new life
精子
In human reproduction, one female egg is usually fertilized by one sperm.
在人体生殖过程中，通常一个精子使一个卵子受精。
He has a low sperm count.
他的精子数很低。
Conception occurs when a single sperm fuses with an egg.
当一个精子与一个卵子交合时就发生受孕。

queue
vi.wait in a line
排队
We had to queue for three hours to get tickets.
我们排了三小时队才买到票。
People are queuing up for fresh fruit.
人们正在排队购买新鲜水果。
n.[C] a line of people, cars, etc. waiting to do sth.
(排队等候的)一队人或车等
There was a long queue of traffic stretching down the road.
车辆排着长长的队，在这条路上延伸。
Jane joined the queue of people waiting for the bus.
简排入等候公共汽车的队伍中。

infertile
a.not able to have children or grow crops
不育的；不结果实的
Many infertile women undergo treatment in the hope that it will help them to have children.
许多不育妇女接受治疗，以期能够生育。
Harvests are poor in this infertile area.
这块贫瘠的土地上收成不好。

artificial
a.1. made by people, often as a copy of sth. natural
人造的，人工的
He was born with only one ear, but surgeons have fitted him with an artificial one.
他生下来时只有一只耳朵，但医生为他装上了一只人造耳。
This drink contains no artificial flavoring or coloring.
这种饮料不掺人造香精或着色剂。
2. lacking true feeling; insincere
假的，矫揉造作的
Their cheerfulness seemed rather strained and artificial.
他们的快乐表情看上去勉强而做作。
She welcomed me with an artificial smile.
她面带不自然的微笑向我表示欢迎。

defect
n.[C] a fault, problem or lack in sth.
缺陷，缺点
I think there are a lot of defects in our educational system.
我认为我们的教育制度中存在许多不足之处。
No person is without defects.
人无完人。

reproduce
v.1. (of humans, animals, etc.) produce young by natural means
（指人类、动物等）繁殖，生殖
If this generation of young women don't start reproducing soon there will be a fall in the population.
如果这一代年轻妇女不赶快开始繁育后代，人口将会下降。
Some creatures were better at surviving and reproducing themselves than others.
有些生物的生存与繁殖后代的能力比其他生物强。
2. produce a copy of sth. or show or do sth. again
复制，再现
They said the painting was too faint to reproduce well.
他们说绘画颜色太浅了，复制不清楚。
The car design unfortunately reproduced some of the problems of the earlier model.
这种汽车设计不幸又重复了前一型号的一些问题。

resign
v.1. be ready to accept sth.
顺从，听从
After his wife died he resigned himself to a lonely old age.
他妻子死后，他顺应天命安于孤独的晚年。
He is resigned to losing the competition.
他只好安于比赛失败的事实。
2. give up (a job or position)
辞职，引退
He resigned in order to take a more challenging job.
他辞去了目前的职务，以便担任一项更具挑战性的工作。
He resigned his position because he had been offered a better job.
他辞职了，因为有人向他提供了一份更好的工作。

dispose
v.1. get rid of sb./sth. that one does not want or cannot keep
摆脱，处理，扔掉
Nuclear waste is often disposed of under the sea.
核废料经常用置入深海的方法来处理。
The murderer was unable to dispose of the body.
凶手无法处理那具尸体。
2. place sb./sth. in a suitable way; arrange
安排，布置
Please dispose your books on the shelves.
请整理架上的图书。
The troops were disposed in battle formation.
部队按照作战队形排列好。

lens
n.[C] a thin piece of glass which usu. makes objects closer, larger, smaller, etc.
透镜，（凹、凸）镜片
You may wear contact lenses to help you see better.
你可以戴隐形眼镜以看得更清楚些。
You may use a zoom or telephoto lens on your camera.
你可以在照相机上安装可变距镜头或摄远镜头。
The lenses of my glasses steamed up in the sudden warmth of the room.
我眼镜上的镜片由于房间突然变暖而蒙上了蒸气。
It was barely visible even through a strong lens.
即使通过高倍透镜也几乎看不见它。

microscope
n.[C] a device that uses lenses to make very small objects look larger
显微镜
Germs are very small and can only be seen with the aid of a microscope.
微生物非常小，只能借助显微镜才看得见。
They are looking at the blood samples under the microscope.
他们正在观察显微镜下的血样。
The latest check will put the company's financial accounts under the microscope.
最近的检查会将公司财务像放在显微镜下一样清楚地显示出来。

democracy
n.1. [C] a country governed by its people or their representatives
民主国家
Few of the Western democracies still have a royal family.
西方民主制国家中很少还有王室的。
In a democracy, every adult has an equal voice in the government.
在一个民主国家，每个成年人在政治上都有平等的发言权。
2. [U] the belief in freedom and equality between people, or a system of government based on this belief
民主；民主政体
The government has promised to support the principles of democracy.
政府已经许诺维持民主原则。
It takes time for true democracy to work.
真正的民主政治的运作需要时间。

temporary
a.not lasting or needed for very long
短暂的，暂时的，临时的
Mobile homes are used as temporary housing for the homeless families.
无家可归的家庭以活动房为临时住处。
Cease-fire only provided a temporary solution to the crisis.
停火只是暂时解决了危机。

systematic
a.done or acting according to a system or plan
有系统的，有计划的，有方法的
He's very systematic in all he does.
他做一切事都有条不紊。
After the systematic investigation and study, they came to a conclusion.
经过系统的调查研究，他们得出了结论。

resume
v.begin (sth.) again or continue (sth.) after stopping for a time
再继续，恢复
He stopped to drink some water and then resumed speaking.
他停下来喝水，然后继续讲话。
Traffic is expected to be resumed shortly.
预计很快就能恢复交通。

layer
n.[C] a level of material
层
There was a thin layer of oil on the surface of the water.
水面上有一层薄的油层。
The study looked at ways to get rid of the present layers of misunderstanding.
该项研究探讨消除目前不同层次的误解的各种方法。

administration
n.1. [U] managing or operating
管理，经营，支配
She has little experience in administration.
她在管理方面没什么经验。
The administration of a big business requires skill in dealing with people.
管理大企业需要谙熟与人打交道的技巧。
2. (sing.) the group of people who organize or control sth.
行政部门，行政机关
the hospital administration
医院的行政部门
They would like the college administration to exert more control over test cheating.
他们希望学校当局加强对考试作弊的控制。
3. (often, the Administration) the government of a country, esp. the USA
政府
the Bush Administration
布什政府
Congress is to raise questions about the administration's handling of the post-Gulf War situation.
国会将就政府对海湾战争后局势的处理提出问题。

govern
v.1. have a controlling influence on; have a direct effect on
影响，支配，规定
Prices of goods are governed by the cost of the raw materials, as well as by the cost of production.
商品的价格受生产成本的影响，同时也受原材料成本的影响。
They are fighting for the right to govern their own lives.
他们为拥有支配自己生活的权利而斗争。
2. control and direct the public business of (a country, city, group of people, etc.); rule
统治，治理，管理
Military leaders have overthrown the president and are now governing the country.
军事领导人把总统赶下了台，现在正统治着这个国家。
Winning elections, and governing well after doing so, are two different activities.
赢得选举和此后治理好国家完全是两回事。

使处于（某种状态），使变得

简的学校成绩报告单使父亲大发雷霆。
