swap
v. exchange (one thing) for another 交换
  e.g. I'll swap my orange for your apple.

  

refer to ... as
mention or describe (sb. or sth.) as 将……称为
  e.g. The professor referred to him as a graduate student with a bright future.

 

bring in
get in 带进来；收获
  e.g. We have brought in a good grain harvest for three years on end.

 

  

gear
n. 个人用品，衣物；装备；齿轮
  e.g. All our camping gear has been packed up.

 

migrate
vi. move from one place to another 迁移
  e.g. In winter, some birds migrate to find warmer weather.

 

  

testimony
n. proof; a declaration by a witness 证明；证词
  e.g. A witness gave testimony that the accused was drunk.

 

saturate
vt. fill completely; soak 使充满；浸透
  e.g. The air was saturated with the perfume of the flowers.

 

   touch on
mention a subject briefly 提起，谈到
  e.g. The book touches on the issue, but only superficially.

 

designate
vt. select and set aside for a specific purpose 选定（或指定）……（作某种用途）
  e.g. He designated Smith as his successor.

 

  

compress
vt. force into less space 压缩
  e.g. He compressed his report into three pages.

 

complain
v. express grief or discontent 悲叹，抱怨
  e.g. Almost immediately he began to complain about the weather.

 

  

to the point
relevant(ly) 相关的（地），切中要害的（地）
  e.g. What he was saying is short and to the point.  

 

look over
examine, inspect 查阅，察看
  e.g. Look over your exercises before handing them in.  

 

 

kick out
expel 赶走，驱逐

 

definition
n. 定义；释义
  e.g. There is no general agreement on a standard definition of intelligence.

 

   spectrum
n. 谱，范围
  e.g. The term "special needs" covers a wide spectrum of problems.

 

dual
a. 双重的
  e.g. He has dual nationality.

 

  

advantage
n. a favorable position or factor, sth. good 优势，优点，好处
  e.g. Mary speaks good English, but she has an advantage because her mother is English.

 

settle down
sit comfortably; become quiet or calm; begin living a stable and orderly life 舒适地坐下；平静下来；定居
  e.g. He settled down to marking the examination papers.

 

  

restless
a. always moving about, unable to remain still 一直在动的， 停不下来的
  e.g. The sick child passed a restless night.
