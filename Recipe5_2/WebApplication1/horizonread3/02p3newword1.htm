come a long way
have developed or become very successful 进展，进步
  e.g. He has come a long way since the days he could afford only a meal a day.

 

  

with each passing year (day, week)
一年一年（一天一天，一星期一星期）地
  e.g. Work becomes harder with each passing day.

 

guarantee
vt. undertake to be legally responsible for 保证；担保
  e.g. Many shopkeepers guarantee satisfaction to customers.

 

  

result from
occur as a result of 因……而发生，是……的结果
  e.g. The problem resulted from past errors.

 

commitment
n. thing one has promised to do 承诺；保证
  e.g. He made a commitment to pay off all his debts.

 

   against (all) the odds
despite strong opposition or disadvantages 尽管极为不利；尽管遭强烈反对
  e.g. Against all the odds she achieved her dream of becoming a journalist.
remarkable
a. worth noticing or unusual 显著的；非凡的
  e.g. Your work has been remarkable this week.

 

  

in the midst of
while (sth.) is happening or being done 在……之中；正当……时候
  e.g. Someone shouted in the midst of his speech.  

recovery
n. return to a normal state 复原；恢复，复苏
  e.g. She made a quick recovery after her illness.

 

  

in this context
against this background; under such circumstances 在这种背景下，在这种情况下
  e.g. We are making reforms, and we must decide our policy in this context.  

 

flourish
vi. be successful, very active, or widespread; prosper 茂盛，繁荣；兴旺
  e.g. The crops flourished in the rich bottomland.

 

  

remedy
vt. correct, change or improve (sth. undesirable) 纠正；消除（弊病等）
  e.g. How can we remedy the situation?

 

be subjected to
cause to experience or undergo 使经历，使遭受
  e.g. They were subjected to great suffering.

 

  

discriminate
vt. treat (one person or group) worse than others 歧视
  e.g. This new law discriminates against lower-paid colored workers.

 
enforce
vt. force people to obey; make (sth.) effective 强迫人服从；实施
  e.g. It isn't easy for the police to enforce speed limits.

 

  

play up
try to make (sth.) appear more important than it is 大肆宣扬；鼓吹
  e.g. He played up his opponent's weak point so much.

 

at best
taking the most hopeful view; at most 最乐观地看；充其量
  e.g. At best a few hundred people attended the opening ceremony.

 

  

unlike
prep. different from; not like 与……不同，不像
  e.g. She was unlike him in every way except for her coal-black eyes.

 

do well (to do sth.)
最好；做得聪明
  e.g. Foreign investors would do well to take a look at China's developing economy.

 

  

grave
a. serious 严重的
  e.g. The sick man's condition is grave.
