die of
死于

 

expense
n. spending of money; cost 花费；代价
  e.g. It's too much of an expense to own a car.

 

  

in control (of sth.)
（对…）有控制力或支配权
  e.g. The Australians are in control of the island.

 

live for
regard as the aim of one's life 将…作为自己的生活目标
  e.g. He lived for his work.

 

  

party
vi. attend or hold social parties 参加（或举办）社交聚会

 

plead
(pleaded or pled) v. give (sth.) as an excuse or explanation, esp. for doing sth. wrong; make repeated urgent requests (for sth.) 提出…作为借口（或理由）；恳求
  e.g. Alice pleaded illness as her reason for being absent.

 

  

plentiful
a. more than enough 大量的，许多的
  e.g. Fish are plentiful in the lake.

 

quit
(quit or quitted) v. stop; give up (one's job); leave 停止；辞去（工作）；离开
  e.g. He quit because the salary was too low.

 

  

reaction
n. 反应
  e.g. He suffered an allergic reaction to penicillin.

 

renew
vt. start (sth.) again; give new energy to 重新开始；使恢复
  e.g. We renewed our discussion after a ten minute break.

 

  

seek after
try to find or obtain 寻找；设法得到
  e.g. Young graduates are seeking after success in life.

 

semester
n. 一学期；半学年
  e.g. She took five courses during the fall semester.

 

 

  

set out to do (sth.)
begin (a job, task, etc.) with a particular aim 着手做（某事）
  e.g. They succeeded in what they set out to do.

 

soak up
take in, absorb 吸收，吸取
  e.g. The paper towel will soak up the spilt coffee.

 

 

  

stir
vt. 搅动，搅和； 激起
  e.g. She stirred the sugar into the mixture.

 

take back
收回；取回
  e.g. I take back what I said.

 

 

  

take/get/gain credit (for sth.)
get honor, praise or approval （因…）获得荣誉（赞誉或认可）
  e.g. I can't take all the credit; the others did all the work.

 

temporary
a. lasting for a short time 暂时的，临时的
  e.g. The temporary worker was soon replaced by a permanent employee.

 

 

  

weigh down
make unhappy and worried, etc. 使忧心忡忡
  e.g. The bank is being weighed down by a $1.3 billion book of bad debts.
