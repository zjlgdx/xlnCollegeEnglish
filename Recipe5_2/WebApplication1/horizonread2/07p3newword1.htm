aspect
n. an element or side of a situation or idea 方面
  e.g. You have only considered one aspect of the problem, but there are many.

 

  

authority
n. 权威人士（或机构）
  e.g. He is an authority on plant diseases.

 

contribute to
help to cause 促成，有助于，是… 的部分原因
  e.g. Various factors contributed to his downfall.

 

  

crude
a. not refined 粗糙的
  e.g. His manners are rather crude.

 

dominate
v. have control of or a very strong influence on 支配， 统治
  e.g. He has authority, but he doesn't try to dominate others.

 

  

economic
a. of an economy or economics 经济（学）的
  e.g. The country is in a bad economic state.

 

exceed
vt. go beyond in quantity, degree, etc. 超过
  e.g. The cost will not exceed $500.

 

  

genuine
a. real, not fake or artificial 真的，非伪造的
  e.g. It's either a genuine diamond or a very good fake.
 

 

give way to
yield to 对…让步，屈服于
  e.g. It seems the President has given way to pressure from hard-liners.

 

  

in transition
in the course of changing into another state or condition 在过渡时期，在转变过程中
  e.g. The company is still in transition from one boss to another.

 

integrate
vt. make into a whole 使成一整体
  e.g. Foreign immigrants here don't integrate well into the white community.

 

  

predict
vt. declare or tell in advance 预言
  e.g. The earthquake was predicted several weeks ago.  

 

professional
n., a. 专业人员（的）
  e.g. You need a professional to sort out your finances.  

 

  

rid oneself of
free oneself from 使自己摆脱
  e.g. The country is making attempts to rid itself of corruption.

 

shift
n.，vi. 改变，变动
  e.g. She hastily shifted the subject.  

 

  

status
n. (high) social position 地位，身份
  e.g. The success of her book has given her unexpected celebrity status.

 

substantial
a. 客观的，有分量的；重大的
  e.g. That is a very substantial improvement in the present situation.

 

 

  

to name a few
to give just a few examples 仅举几例
  e.g. Lots of our friends are coming: Anne, Ken and George, to name but a few.

 

trend
n. general tendency or direction 趋势
  e.g. It is obvious from the graph that there is an upward trend in coal production.  

 

  

unique
a. being the only one of its kind 独特的
  e.g. None of these social problems are unique to this country.
