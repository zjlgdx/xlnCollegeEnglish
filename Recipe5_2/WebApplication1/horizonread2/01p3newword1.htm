access
n. 享用（或接近、进入）的机会
  e.g. Every student has free access to the library.

  

account
n. arrangement made with a bank to pay in or take out money; description 账，账户; 描述，记述
  e.g. Will you add the cost of this skirt to my account as I haven't any money with me?

 

adopt
vt. take and use as one's own 采纳，采取
  e.g. He adopted his friend's ideas.

 

  

annual
a. happening or done every year or once a year 每年的；一年一次的
  e.g. Here's the annual report of this company, read through it and you'll know better about the company.

 

deposit
n. a sum of money paid into a bank account 存款
v. put (money) into a bank account 储蓄
  e.g. I'd like to make a deposit, please.

 

  

devise
vt. think out; invent 想出，设计；发现
  e.g. They devised a plan for getting the jewels out of the country.

 

end up (doing sth.)
find oneself in a situation that is not intended or expected 以…而告终
  e.g. If you don't know what you want, you might end up getting something you don't want.

 

   fall apart
break, fall to pieces 破裂；破碎
  e.g. He fell apart after years as a POW(prisoner of war).

 

from one's point of view
依照某人的观点
  e.g. The exercise is worth the cost from my point of view.

 

  

fund
n. (usu.pl) sum of money saved or for a particular purpose 存款；基金; 专款
  e.g. His parents set up a fund for his college education.

 

furthermore
ad. in addition, moreover 此外，而且
  e.g. The house is too small, and furthermore, it's expensive.

 

  

intend
vt. have in mind as a purpose or plan 打算
  e.g. Nobody knows what David intends to do after graduation.  

 

keep track of
follow the development of; keep in touch with 跟上…的发展；与…保持联系
  e.g. Part of your task is to keep track of the business performance of that company.  

 

 

on demand
when demanded 一经要求，承索
  e.g. Passengers must show their ticket on demand.

 

pace
n. speed or rate of development, movement, etc. 速度
  e.g. The old man walked at a very slow pace.

 

   perspective
n. a particular way of thinking about sth. 视角，观点
  e.g. The two attitudes derive from different historical perspectives.

plus
prep. with the addition of 加，加上
  e.g. Two plus five is seven.

 

  

protest
vi. express strong disagreement or disapproval about (sth.) 抗议，反对
  e.g. Customers are protesting against higher prices.

 

resource
n. (usu. pl) 财力；资源
  e.g. That country is rich in natural resources.

 

  

undoubtedly
ad. certainly 无疑，必定
  e.g. This is undoubtedly a good chance for improving your language skill.
