acknowledge
v. admit or recognize sth. 承认
  e.g. They refused to acknowledge that they were defeated.

  

aware
a. having knowledge or experience of sth. 知道的，明白的
  e.g. He doesn't seem to be aware of the risk.

 

beneath
prep. under 在…之下
  e.g. The cat was beneath the chair.
 

 

  

contrary
a. completely different 相反的
to the contrary 相反的（地）
  e.g. In spite of all the evidence to the contrary, Holmes was convinced the woman was innocent.

 

define
vt. explain what sth. is 定义，详细说明
  e.g. The teacher defined each confusing word for the students.
 

 

  

disturb
v. upset 使苦恼，使不安，扰乱
  e.g. If she's asleep, don't disturb her.

 

embrace
v., n. 拥抱
  e.g. They embraced each other tenderly on the station platform.

 

  

have sth. to oneself
独自拥有，独自使用
  e.g. With my parents away I've got the house all to myself.

 

justify
v. show that sth. is necessary, or that there is a good reason for sth. 证明…是正当的
  e.g. He could not justify his absence from class this morning.

 

  

legal
a. allowed by law 合法的，法定的
  e.g. The children's legal rights must be protected.
 

 

let go of
放手；放开
  e.g. Don't let go of the handle.

 

  

look forward to
期待，盼望
  e.g. The children are looking forward to the Spring Festival.

 

mood
n. 心情，情绪
  e.g. Don't disturb him. He's in a very bad mood.

 

  

notion
n. an idea 观念，想法
  e.g. He had a sudden notion to leave.

 

polish
vt. make smooth, bright and shiny by continual rubbing 擦亮
  e.g. They polished the car.

 

  

rare
a. not often found or done 稀罕的
  e.g. It's very rare for her to be late.

 

respond
v. reply 回答
  e.g. She responded to my letter with a phone call.

 

  

sake
n. reason 缘故，理由
for one's sake out of consideration for; for the benefit of 为了…的缘故；为了…的利益
  e.g. He changed into old shoes for the sake of comfort.
